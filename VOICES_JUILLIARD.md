## VOICE CONSTRUCTION FOR JUILLIARD SCENES

### Group Settings (Performances, Master Classes, After-Practice)

**Register shift:** More verbal. Technical discussion. Conversations that assume deep musical knowledge.

**What distinguishes them from other friend groups:** They talk about chord voicings, technique, interpretation. They assess each other's work with a frankness that would be rude in other contexts but is normal here.

**When Alex enters:** No worship. Recognition of his talent (he's impressive at an elite music school). Interest in what he's working on.

### One-On-One (Studios, Private Lessons, Coffee)

**Nora:** Asks real questions. Technical talk. Friendship has depth.

**Marcus:** Enthusiastic. Will compliment Alex's work and mean it. Sometimes over-the-top but sincere.

**Derrick:** Late night sessions. Music-first. Minimal small talk.

**Sasha:** Quiet. Shared practice room time. Comfortable silence.

**Jin:** Asks for feedback. Shares new compositions. Trusts Alex's ear.

**Marlowe:** Teaching mode. Precise. No small talk.

**Osei:** Engaged about voice work. Believes in Alex.

## CRITICAL NOTES

**Do not write Juilliard peers as starstruck.** They are world-class musicians. Talented people are normal here. What they react to is good music, not Rosie's grandson.

**Do not write them as ignoring Alex's background.** They know who he is. It's just not the thing that makes them friends — the music is.

**Do not homogenize their voices.** Nora is direct-technical. Marcus is enthusiastic-performer. Derrick is minimal-focused. Sasha is quiet-intense. Jin is thoughtful-composer. Let them be distinct.

**Do not over-perform musicianship.** They don't talk about music every moment. They're young people in New York going to dinners, making plans, living lives. Music is central, not exclusive.

**Do not write them as inferior to the Circle.** These are elite musicians in a world-class conservatory. They are peers with Alex in the music world, different in that they're Alex's age and generation instead of his teachers' generation.
