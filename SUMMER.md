# THE EUROPEAN SUMMER CIRCUIT — SOCIAL GEOGRAPHY

**The annual circuit as it actually runs. Reference for: where Alex is in any given week, who's around him, how the pap situation changes by location, which group chats go active, and what the media sees.**

---

## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

These are not Alex's haunts. They are independent social worlds that run whether he shows up or not. Glastonbury happens every year regardless. Porto Cervo fills every July regardless. The Hamptons empties from NYC every August regardless. Alex is a node in each of these ecosystems — sometimes a significant one, sometimes just another person at the beach. The crossover is organic, not Alex-centric.

Where his circles intersect each world is noted. Where they don't, that's noted too.

---

## STOP 1 — COMPOUND (Late May → Early June)

**Timing:** 1-3 weeks after end of academic year. The decompress.

**Who's there:** Tommy, the Sayre Boys, Maria's food, Homer's studio, Rosie. The people who require nothing.

**What happens:** Running, piano, dinners at the Crawfords', lake weekends at Cahill's place on Herrington, Sayre Boys poker, maybe a Hatfield dinner. The circuit hasn't started yet. This stretch doesn't feel like part of the summer — it feels like before the summer starts.

**Paps:** Woodford County baseline. No permanent pack but not zero. Outside the compound gate: assume cameras eventually. Inside the gate: nothing.

**Chats active:** Sayre Boys (daily), Family, Phoenix Hollow (warmth, Stevie sending food photos, Circle checking in).

**Media profile:** Low. Unless something news-cycle-worthy pulls focus, tabloid machines tick over on older content. The compound stretch is a relative blackout.

---

## STOP 2 — NYC (Early-Mid June)

**Timing:** Usually mid-June, after a Kentucky stretch, before Glastonbury.

**The townhouse:** West 70s, Upper West Side. Six-story brownstone. Alex's home for three Juilliard years — he shaped every room. Not visiting. Returning.

**Who's there:** NYC Lot (Nora, Marcus, Sasha, Jin, Derrick); Zero Bond and Casa Cipriani regulars; Henry Cabot; Sam Okafor; whoever from the wider orbit is passing through.

**What happens:** Late nights at Zero Bond. Hatfield's West Village when he wants quieter. Dinners at Casa Cipriani or The Carlyle. Occasional music things — someone's concert, someone's show, late-night session. Art gallery openings. Walking to coffee at 9am and generating tabloid content without trying.

**Paps:** NYC is serious. Permanent rotating press pack. 8-15 photographers on a normal morning outside the townhouse. More after sightings. Multiple agencies (Getty, Splash, Backgrid, MEGA) with standing assignments.

**Companion identification:** Any new face photographed near Alex in NYC has their name, Instagram, and connection to Alex surfaced within hours.

**Chats active:** New York Lot ("Tonight?"), Summer House (picking up), Legacy Admissions.

**Media profile:** High baseline, spiking after anything.

## STOP 3 — LONDON (Mid-June, and again after Sardinia if needed)

**Timing:** Usually mid-to-late June, often before or after Glastonbury.

**The house:** Upper Phillimore Gardens, Kensington. Homer's place. Six floors. Mrs. Gable has it ready. Alex's suite is on the top floor. He has lived in this house for two complete years of his life (Westminster + RAM year). He is not a visitor.

**Who's there:** The London Lot in full — Freddie, Jess, Cosima, Hugo, cousins, Westminster Boys, Annabel's crowd, Iris, Lila, Rocco, Lennon, Rufus King and the polo set, Lady Louise.

**What happens:** Dinners at the Kensington house. Annabel's. Chiltern Firehouse. The Stave (the family's private members club). Runs in Hyde Park. Saturday afternoon polo at Guards. Gallery openings. Chelsea if there's a match. Wimbledon: Middle Sunday, clients' enclosure, Aorangi Terrace.

**Paps:** Routes fully mapped — the walk from Phillimore Gardens to Kensington Church Street, the route to Hyde Park. Not the industrial NYC volume but professional, organised, persistent. Backgrid and Splash run London particularly hard.

**Chats active:** London Lot (explodes the moment he lands), Westminster Boys, Dawson Cousins, Summer House.

**Media profile:** High. Daily Mail and The Sun treat his London presence as standing content.

## STOP 4 — GLASTONBURY (Late June, Most Years)

**Timing:** Last weekend of June. Alex has been most years since mid-teens.

**The logistics:** Private transport to Worthy Farm. Stays in backstage hospitality or with Circle members performing. General festival access when he wants to move through public areas — paps, fans, phone cameras at festival scale.

**Who's there:** Summer House chat members, London Lot people, Legacy Kids, Rocco, Lennon, Cara, Raye if performing, whoever from Phoenix Hollow is playing.

**What he does:** Watches sets. Plays something informally — usually a piano somewhere. Late nights in hospitality. Genuinely enjoys it. Glastonbury is one of the places where he's most clearly having a good time.

**Paps:** Festival-scale ecosystem. Public areas are essentially impossible to control. Fan phone cameras create a secondary layer. Getty photographers embedded with press credentials. Tabloids run Glastonbury content all weekend.

**Chats active:** Summer House (peak chaos), London Lot (many overlap), Legacy Admissions.

**Media profile:** Reliable tabloid content. Companion identification runs hot. Any woman photographed near him becomes a rumoured girlfriend.

## STOP 5 — MILAN (Some Years, Not Every Year)

**Timing:** Variable. Usually July, a few days.

**Why Milan:** Donatella. She dressed him since he was ten. A summer visit is not business obligation — it's family. They have dinner. She shows him new things. He listens to her opinion on everything.

**The secondary Milan:** The European circle — Tommaso (Milan-based when not in Sardinia), Camille, Edo, others. A Milan stop is often the warm-up to Sardinia.

**Paps:** Lower volume than London or NYC but professional and organised. Italian paparazzi love him — handsome, multilingual, speaks perfect Italian, Versace connection. Consistent content.

**Chats active:** La Terrazza (activates ahead of Sardinia, Milan is where the trip gets planned), Summer House.

---

## STOP 6 — SARDINIA (Every July, Two Weeks)

**Timing:** Mid-July, consistently. Two weeks. Costa Smeralda.

**The trip:** Most relaxed stretch of the entire summer. The La Terrazza group — Tommaso, Camille, Edo, Lucía, Félix, Giulia Agnelli, whoever from Summer House is coming. Private villa or boat. Porto Cervo as base. La Terrazza the restaurant as anchor.

**Who's there:** The European circle (La Terrazza chat). Some London Lot overlap — Iris, Rocco, Hugo occasionally. Legacy people occasionally — Kaia, Maya, Jacob Elordi for a few days. Most international, least English-language stretch. Texts primarily in Italian and French.

**What happens:** Days on the water. Long lunches. Late dinners that don't start until 10pm. Swimming. Boat trips to Cala di Volpe or Capriccioli. Very little agenda.

**Paps:** **Virtually no cameras.** The Costa Smeralda has a particular privacy culture — pap presence genuinely minimal compared to London or NYC. Not zero (boat photographers work Sardinia in July) but incomparably lower. He's not followed on the water. Not photographed at dinner most nights. This is why it's non-negotiable.

**Chats active:** La Terrazza (peak activity). Summer House (people messaging from outside wanting updates, Alex barely posting).

**Media profile:** Minimal. An occasional shot from a distance. Tabloids know Sardinia is the quiet stretch.

## STOP 7 — IBIZA (July-August)

**Timing:** Variable, usually one stretch not two weeks. Less fixed than Sardinia.

**Two worlds on one island:**

1. **Music:** DJ residency circuit. DC-10 on Mondays, Amnesia, Pacha. Sunset sessions. The living institutional history of electronic music.

2. **Boats and villas:** Money that followed the music. Private yachts, villas in the hills. Club world and villa world party together after 2am.

**Edo Ferretti** is the most important person in Alex's world at Ibiza. Edo knows the island the way Tommaso knows Porto Cervo — not as a tourist but as someone whose social world has been shaped by it.

**Who's here:** Edo, Summer House chat (peak activation), various London/NYC crossover.

**Alex here:** Passes through rather than commits. Usually one stretch, not two weeks. More going out and volume being high than Villa Serena pace. He likes it in controlled doses. Not his territory the way it's Edo's.

**Paps:** Town (Ibiza Town and Sant Antoni) has photographers. Clubs are dark inside. Boat days are private. Villa hills are difficult terrain.

## STOP 8 — LA (Late July, Some Years)

**Timing:** Late July, sometimes August. Not every year — depends on story.

**The house:** Laurel Canyon. Rosie and Homer's home 1967-1980. The Circle formed here. Home studio. Not his house the way NYC townhouse is — a family property he stays in.

**Why go:** Legacy circles are LA-based. Kaia, Maya, Zoë, Margaret, Maude, Jacob. The Laurel Canyon house has specific music energy: the home studio, the history, the vibe of the Canyon in summer.

**What happens:** Dinners at Chateau Marmont or Delilah. Sessions at the house studio (informal). Some film-adjacent socialising. Hiking in the Canyon. San Vicente Bungalows. Less structured than London or NYC.

**Paps:** LA pap culture is different — more car-based, more highway pursuit. Industrial but different. The Chateau is well-covered. The Canyon itself is less papped than Robertson Boulevard.

**Chats active:** Legacy Admissions (most active when he's in LA), Summer House (California contingent wakes up).

---

## STOP 9 — HAMPTONS (Overlapping, Late July–August)

**Timing:** Bleeds into NYC circuit, late July and August.

**Alex's take on it:** "Not favourite — too performative." He goes because people he likes are there.

**Who's there:** Henry Cabot typically has a house. Zero Bond crowd migrates east. Some Summer House people. NYC circuit and LA-NYC-London circuit collision point.

**What happens:** Pool days. Dinners. Beach. The occasional nightlife stop (Surf Lodge, etc.). Tabloid-friendly.

**Paps:** Well-covered. Celebrity content machine runs hard. Less urban-density than NYC but car-following common.

---

## STOP 10 — BACK TO WOODFORD COUNTY (August)

**Timing:** Late August. The circuit winds down.

**The summer resolution:** Months of noise and movement and perpetual visibility collapse into the compound again. Tommy is there. Maria's food. The tobacco barn. August is quieter than June.

## THE SUMMER CIRCUIT AS MACHINE

The circuit is a fixed thing — it runs every year. The timing doesn't change much. The places don't change much. The people don't change much. It's a machine, and Alex has been riding it since he was a teenager.

This summer is different because he's riding it for the first time without the weight of school — but also for the first time with the full weight of post-graduation industry attention. The machine that used to feel liberating now feels entangled. The places that used to be escapes now feel like stages.

The question the story is holding: Can Alex have the summer he always has while also dealing with the machinery that's supposed to define his next decade? Or does the machinery reshape the summer?

The circuit doesn't care about the answer. It runs.
