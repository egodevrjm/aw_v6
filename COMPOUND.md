# PHOENIX HOLLOW COMPOUND

**Woodford County, Kentucky | ~500 acres | $56M estate | Private family home since 1980**

---

## GEOGRAPHY & CALIBRATION

Phoenix Hollow is in Woodford County, Kentucky — **not Appalachia, not rural poverty, not country music territory**. Woodford County is horse country, one of the wealthiest agricultural regions in America. Think Surrey's Cotswolds with thoroughbreds, not a holler with a pickup truck. The cars are nice. The restaurants are good. The people are educated and wealthy.

> **CRITICAL NOTE:** For comprehensive Woodford County calibration — regional economics, horse culture, social hierarchy, dining/bar scene, local geography (Versailles, Lexington, Sayre, Natural Bridge) — **see WOODFORD.md**. This file covers only the compound's physical spaces and internal operations.

The compound is the only genuinely private place in Alex's life. Inside the gate: no cameras, no press, total privacy. Outside the gate: Woodford County is small, everyone knows where the Walkers live, and paparazzi have been around Versailles and near Sayre since Alex was a child. The gate is the line.

## MAIN HOUSE (~12,000 sqft)

**Kentucky vernacular — stone, hardwood, big windows. 6 bedrooms. Lived-in, not showpiece.**

### Ground Floor

**Kitchen**
- Long table seats 12. Heart of everything. Maria's domain. Coffee is always made. The table is where conversations happen — not the formal dining room, not the great room. The kitchen.

**Music Room**
- Rosie's room. 1923 Steinway Model D. Soundproofed. Morning light through the windows. This is where Alex first touched a piano. The Steinway's action is what his hands know best — every other piano is compared to this one.

**Library**
- Homer's room. Floor-to-ceiling shelves. The chair Homer reads in. The desk he doesn't use.

**Great Room**
- High ceilings, stone fireplace. The room for when people gather — Circle visits, holidays, after the Solstice. Not where daily life happens.

**Media Room**
- Screening, casual TV. Where Alex and Tommy watch games.

**Wraparound Porch**
- Rocking chairs. The view south over the paddocks. Where Rosie sits in the evening. Where conversations happen in summer without anyone deciding to have a conversation.

### Second Floor

**Alex's Suite**
- Corner of the house. Main bedroom — windows on two sides overlooking the south paddock and the creek tree line. An upright piano against the interior wall for morning practice before the phone goes on. Books stacked on the nightstand, the floor, the windowsill. A guitar leaning by the door. The room hasn't been redecorated since he was a teenager — he hasn't been home long enough to care. Comfortable, lived-in, slightly chaotic. 
- Adjoining guest room (overflow for books, gear, whatever accumulates). 
- Room-sized walk-in closet — fully stocked, every season, every house sends here first. The contrast between the unmade bed and the organised closet is Alex in miniature. 
- En-suite bathroom — large, nothing fancy, the bath is the thing (freestanding, original to the house renovation, where he reads).

**Rosie & Homer's Master Suite**
- Rarely occupied since they retired full-time at the compound. Maintained, always ready.

**2 Additional Bedrooms**
- Guest quarters for visiting family.

### Basement

**Gym**
- Full-size. Free weights, machines, mat space. Where Alex trains when he's home.

## ART — MAIN HOUSE

This is the true family home, so the most deeply personal portraits and gifts from musical titans reside here. No labels, nothing hung for show.

### Personal Portraits

- **Andy Warhol:** 1973 diptych portrait of Rosie Walker. Alex grew up eating breakfast down the hall from it — to him it's not pop art, it's just his grandmother.
- **Richard Avedon:** 1971 portrait of Rosie.
- **Herb Ritts:** 1989 portrait of Rosie — stark, cinematic natural light during the post-retirement era.
- **Martin Schoeller:** 2011 extreme close-up of Alex at age 8. Shot in Schoeller's unsparing style with fluorescent catchlights, isolating his storm-blue eyes long before the current fame.

### Gifts & Installations

- **Cy Twombly:** Massive looping chalkboard canvas spanning an entire wall of the Great Room. The custom steel framing hidden in the wall required serious commercial construction engineering — the kind Tommy's dad's company does all the time. Tommy is probably the only person who's noticed.
- **Joni Mitchell:** Original oil landscape of Creek Cottage, painted by Mitchell during a stay in the mid-1990s.
- **Bob Dylan:** Welded iron gate from salvaged tools and scrap metal, made in Dylan's Malibu studio, gifted to Rosie. Stands at the entrance to the kitchen garden.
- **Stevie Nicks:** Polaroid diptych — large, blurry, deeply atmospheric Polaroid transfers from the 1988 Solstice party.
- **David Hockney:** *The Pool at Phoenix Hollow* — massive sun-drenched canvas depicting the compound pool, reimagining Kentucky light as a Los Angeles afternoon. Hangs casually in the basement gym.
- **The Letter:** Framed unassumingly in Homer's library — handwritten note on White House stationery from Barack Obama, thanking them for the hospitality following Alex's 21st birthday party.

### Art — Alex's Suite

- **Maude Apatow 35mm Candid:** On the nightstand. Grainy black-and-white of Alex, Lila, and Kaia completely asleep in the back of a water taxi in Sardinia. The only photo of himself he displays, purely because of the people in it.
- **Tommy's "Trophy":** Next to the Patek on the dresser — an absurdly unflattering Polaroid of Alex at 15, covered in mud from a Sayre School camping trip. Tommy gave it for his 18th with strict instructions it must remain on display "to keep your ego from getting out of control." Alex genuinely loves it.
- **Wolfgang Tillmans Print (gift from Elton):** Massive, unframed, of a crowded, sweaty, euphoric Berlin club scene. Elton gave it for Alex's 18th with a note: "stop practicing scales and go be a teenager." Hangs above the upright practice piano.

## THE SOLSTICE PARTIES

**The most exclusive events in entertainment.** Think Met Gala meets Elton's Oscar parties — but on 500 private acres with a natural amphitheatre, guest cottages, no cameras, no phones, no press. Nothing leaks. No photos exist outside the compound. The industry knows about them the way it knows about Bohemian Grove — everyone's heard, nobody outside has seen.

### Summer Solstice (June, ~200+ guests)

The big one. People fly private into Blue Grass Airport. The four guest cottages fill first, then hotels in Lexington and Versailles. Music in the amphitheatre — Rosie has performed at every one since 1981. Not a concert — spontaneous, collaborative, whoever's there picks up an instrument. Stevie and Rosie singing together at dusk. Paul on a borrowed guitar. Beyoncé joining in. The only place on earth where this happens. Catered but relaxed — Maria's team plus outside help, long tables, bourbon, Kentucky summer evening. It runs all night. Some years there's been a dawn set. Alex has been at every one since birth.

### Winter Solstice / Christmas (~60-80 guests)

Smaller, warmer, more intimate. The real inner circle. Fires, the main house, mulled everything. More domestic than the summer version — people stay longer, days not hours.

## WRITING NOTES

**Familiarity, not novelty.** Alex doesn't notice the art on the wall — it's been there his whole life. He doesn't admire the view from the porch — he's seen it ten thousand times. He notices what's DIFFERENT today. A new face. A moved chair. The light at an angle he hasn't seen because he hasn't been home in May for five years.

**Art is furniture.** The Warhol diptych of Rosie is not "a Warhol" to Alex — it's the painting he's walked past since he could walk. The Twombly in the Great Room is the big one with the chalk. Tommy's Polaroid on the dresser matters more to him emotionally than the Hockney in the gym. Write the art as background unless a specific piece is doing narrative work.

**The compound is not rural.** It's a $56M estate with a private chef from Per Se, a Neve console, and four guest cottages that have housed McCartney and Beyoncé. No "country boy" framing. No "simple pleasures." The $400 jeans and Loro Piana hoodie ARE his casual.

**Maria's food is not Southern.** She is CIA/Per Se/French Laundry trained. The food at Phoenix Hollow is tasting-menu quality with global range — refined French technique, Italian simplicity, seasonal produce from the greenhouse. Not cornbread, not biscuits, not fried chicken, not anything that codes "Kentucky home cooking." Alex eats Southern bar food *out* at Hatfield's in Lexington. At home, he eats what a world-class chef makes. These are different things. Do not conflate them.

**Each house has a different piano, a different feel.** The Steinway D at the compound is what his hands know best. The Model B in NYC is brighter, more intimate. The Bechstein in London has a different action entirely. A pianist notices. Write the difference.

**Each house has different art, different energy.** NYC is cool and downtown. Kensington is heavy and British. The compound is warm and personal. Laurel Canyon is sun-drenched and historical. Don't mix the aesthetics.
