# THE NEW YORK LOT

**Alex's New York social circle: Juilliard musicians, Zero Bond regulars, fashion, film, music-industry people, and the broader NYC social infrastructure. Full profiles of how they know each other and how this world works.**

---

## WHAT THE NEW YORK LOT IS

The New York Lot is approximately 40-50 people who are part of the same general social ecosystem in Manhattan. Rich people, art people, music people, actors, industry people, fashion people — all based in New York most of the time. They see each other at Zero Bond, at Casa Cipriani, at gallery openings in Chelsea, at concerts at Zankel Hall, at dinners that become nights that become 3am somewhere downtown. They know each other because New York's elite social world is simultaneously vast and small — you end up in the same rooms.

This group is not built around Alex. It existed before him and will exist without him. Alex entered it through Juilliard at 18, through Lincoln Center proximity, through the natural gravitational pull of being young, talented, and present in Manhattan for three years. His Juilliard peers are the core of his personal connection to the NYL, but the group is much larger than the music world — it includes finance people, models, actors, creatives, people who simply live in New York and move at this register.

The New York Lot is distinct from the London Lot (which is older money, more structured, driven by the seasonal calendar and educational infrastructure) and from the LA Lot (which is industry-adjacent, sun-soaked, driven by the entertainment calendar). New York is spontaneous. New York is "tonight?" at 6pm becoming twelve people at Zero Bond by 9pm. New York is a bodega at 2am after Casa Cipriani. New York moves fast and doesn't wait.

## JUILLIARD CORE

These are the people Alex knows from three years at Juilliard. They share a particular bond: they are world-class musicians who assessed each other on the basis of talent, not fame. Their connection to Alex is through music first.

### NORA YOON

Alex's closest Juilliard friend. Direct, funny, impatient with anyone performing seriousness rather than having it. Knows his playing like nobody except Marlowe. When Nora says something about his music, he listens — not because she's diplomatic (she isn't), but because she's right.

**Her world:** Professional-track violinist. Post-graduation life is auditions, competitions, freelance orchestral work, teaching to make rent. **Relationship to Alex:** Musical peer and genuine friend. They can spend an evening talking about voicings and interpretation or they can spend it not talking about music at all. The ease is real.

## THE NEW YORK LOT IS NOT THE ALEX CHANNEL

The same principle as the London Lot, amplified by New York's speed:

1. **The group has its own life.** Plans formed while Alex was in Kentucky. Threads about a gallery opening he'll never see. A debate about a restaurant that happened at 2am. The NYL was active before he opened his phone. Multiple conversations are always happening simultaneously.

2. **Vary the cast every time.** The named profiles above are the most developed. They are not the only people in the room. The NYL has 40-50 members. On any given Tuesday: a Juilliard grad is complaining about a rehearsal. A model is posting from a shoot in Brooklyn. Henry has a table. Someone's asking about a comedy show. A finance person nobody's heard from in weeks has a take about a restaurant. Generate the other 25 people.

3. **Generate unnamed voices.** Not everyone in the NYL has a profile. When rendering, generate additional voices — a photographer called Miles who posts from parties, someone who works at a gallery in Chelsea, a friend-of-Marcus who got added to the chat after a memorable night at Casa Cipriani and is now a regular presence. These people don't need entries. They need to exist.

4. **Alex is not the centre of gravity when he's absent.** Since graduation, he's been in Kentucky. The NYL continued without him. When he opens the chat, there should be days of conversation he missed, plans that happened, inside jokes from nights he wasn't at. The group functions without him.

5. **The economic range is real.** The NYL includes Henry Cabot (old money, always has a table) and Derrick Washington (jazz musician, teaching gigs to make rent). This contrast is present without anyone commenting on it. The bodega tier and the Casa Cipriani tier coexist. Nobody is uncomfortable about this because New York makes it normal.

## KEY DYNAMICS

### How the NYL Functions

Speed. That's the defining characteristic. A Monday nothing becomes a plan involving twelve people by 7pm. Somebody always knows somebody who knows about something happening tonight. The architecture of spontaneity — nobody plans more than four hours ahead, but somehow things coalesce.

The venues: Zero Bond (private members, downtown), Casa Cipriani (private members, Financial District), Zankel Hall (concerts), the downtown gallery circuit, late-night restaurants, the bodega at 2am, somebody's apartment rooftop in the West Village. The city itself is the venue — the group moves through it rather than gathering in one place.

### Seasonality

New York has seasons: September through May is the active period. Late spring energy is different from midwinter energy. Summer the city empties — people scatter to the Hamptons, to Europe, to wherever the Summer House chat is coordinating. The NYL chat goes quieter in July and August, then reactivates in September with returning-to-the-city energy.

### Pap Dynamics

New York paps are different from London. More aggressive. More organised. Specific locations are well-covered (Nobu, Carbone, Zero Bond entrance, certain SoHo blocks). The dynamic changes depending on who's present — Alex alone is one thing; Alex with Timothée or the Hadids is another calculus entirely.

Private members clubs have no-photography policies. Inside is safe. The walk to the car is not.

### The Juilliard-to-Social Pipeline

Alex's unique position: he entered the NYL through Juilliard, which means his core NYC friendships are with musicians. But through three years of living in Manhattan, through the Mercer Street townhouse, through the natural social gravity of being who he is, the circle expanded to include the Zero Bond crowd, the fashion world, the actors, the broader infrastructure. The Juilliard core remains the deepest connection. The wider NYL is where the spontaneous Thursday night happens.

## NARRATOR NOTES

The New York Lot is the social world where Alex's music life and his social life intersect most naturally. In London, his social world is education-driven. In Kentucky, it's childhood. In LA, it's industry. In New York, it's everything at once — the musician, the socialite, the kid at the bodega at 2am, the person who gets photographed walking into Zero Bond.

New York is also the city where the economic contrast is starkest. Alex lives in a $22M townhouse five blocks from Lincoln Center. Nora Yoon is trying to make rent. Both are in the same group chat. Both go to the same concerts. This is New York — it contains multitudes and doesn't apologise for the contradictions.

Since graduation, Alex's presence in the NYL has faded from daily to occasional. He's in Kentucky. But the relationships are real, the chat is still active, and when he's back in the city — the townhouse is there, the friends are there, the midnight plan is always forming.
