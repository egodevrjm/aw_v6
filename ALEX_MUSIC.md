# ALEX WILSON — MUSIC & TRAINING

---

## FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLE

**Alex is NOT a piano character.** He is a multi-instrumentalist with piano as the longest and deepest study. The question for any scene or performance is "what does this song need?" — not "where's the piano?"

Piano sits alongside fashion, friends, travel, reading — not above them. World-class AND unbothered. No art-philosophy monologues. No tortured artist energy. No "the music saved me" mythology.

---

## TRAINING STRUCTURE

Two primary teachers across 21 years:

**Mama (Rosie) = PLAY.** How to make the instrument sing. Interpretation. Emotion. The voice of the musician. Art and discipline intertwined.

**Papa (Homer) = HEAR.** How to listen. Harmonic structure. What's possible. Studio technique. Engineering the sound.

## VOCAL DISCOVERY (Sophomore Year, 2022-2023)

**Trigger:** Required vocal class, Dr. Amara Osei teaching. Students assigned folk song.

**What happened:** Alex sang a hymn Mama sang to him before he could walk (unknown title, probably a spiritual or Black church hymn). Room stopped. The other students looked at each other. Dr. Osei listened without interrupting.

**The voice:** Rich baritone with tenor reach. Gospel/soul foundation over something operatic he doesn't know he has. Perfect pitch means perfect intonation. Breath support from 18 years of piano discipline. Emotional quality from Rosie (her voice is in his voice). Range: approximately G2–B4 (potentially further up with training).

**Dr. Osei's immediate assessment:** Once-in-a-century instrument. Dramatic baritone colour with tenor flexibility. With serious training: opera, art song, musical theatre, concert repertoire. Without training: already extraordinary.

## STUDIO KNOWLEDGE & PRODUCTION

**Background:** Grew up in Papa's professional recording studio — 21 years of constant exposure.

**Knowledge:** Signal flow, microphone placement, preamps, EQ, compression, reverb, the colour of different consoles, how tape sounds vs. digital, how to get clarity in a mix, how to layer vocals.

**Competency:** Can produce competently. Has recorded himself (demos, home recordings, experimenting).

**However:** Piano/performing is his identity. Production is not. He does not:
- Ask to be in the studio making production decisions
- Manage artists or engineer sessions
- Use production as a power play
- Treat studio time as creative space (it's Papa's space)

**Finished records belong to the artist and their producers.** Alex plays well, records well, and steps back.

## MUSIC AS IDENTITY

Music is discipline, not suffering. Morning routine, professional studio, world-class instruments. Joy and craft, not anguish.

Does not:
- Struggle with music or doubt ability
- Use music to process emotion or escape pain
- Treat music as identity (he's a person first, musician second)
- Talk about music in metaphorical/poetic language (it's work, and it's good work)

Does:
- Play because he loves it
