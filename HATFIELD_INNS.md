# HATFIELD INNS

---

## THE OPENING PRINCIPLE

**Hatfield Inns are boutique hotels that exist because someone who owns a bourbon distillery and a restaurant chain eventually realises guests need somewhere to sleep.**

These are not luxury resorts. Not budget hotels. Not Airbnb competitors. Hatfield Inns are mid-to-upper-range boutique hotels with a bourbon hospitality identity — comfortable rooms, quality food and drink (often connected to or adjacent to a Hatfield Bar & Grill), and the kind of warmth that comes from a brand rooted in Southern hospitality rather than corporate optimisation.

Alex doesn't stay at Hatfield Inns — he has homes. But he knows they exist, he's walked through a few, and he understands they're part of the family business. Visitors to Bardstown for distillery tours, guests in Louisville for the Derby, business travellers who prefer character over chain — that's the Hatfield Inns guest.

## THE CONCEPT

Hatfield Inns were born from the distillery tourism pipeline. Visitors came to Bardstown for bourbon, ate at the Bar & Grill, then drove home or stayed at a generic chain hotel. The first Hatfield Inn (Bardstown, adjacent to the distillery) solved that problem: give bourbon tourists a place to stay that extended the Hatfield experience.

The concept evolved. Not every location is tied to bourbon tourism — Louisville and Nashville serve urban travellers and event-goers. But the identity remains consistent: warm, characterful, bourbon-aware hospitality that feels like a proper place rather than a room number.

**Design language:**
- Timber, stone, leather, warm lighting. Kentucky aesthetic translated for each market.
- Each property designed individually (not a cookie-cutter template). Local architects, local materials where possible.
- Bourbon bar in every lobby. Not a full bar — a curated bourbon selection with comfortable seating for evening pours.
- No minibar bourbon (bad optics for a distillery brand). Instead, each room has a bourbon tasting card — guests can request a curated tasting delivered to their room.
- Quality bedding, quality bathrooms, functional workspace. Not flashy. Comfortable.

## EXPANSION

### Company-Owned Pipeline (2026-2028)

Selective company-owned expansion in premium markets where brand control matters:

- **Napa Valley, CA** — Adjacent to Hatfield Wines estate. Wine country + bourbon. Under evaluation.
- **Paris, France** (longer-term) — Luxury boutique market. Higher price point, smaller property.
- **Tokyo, Japan** (longer-term) — Paired with The Stave Tokyo expansion.

### Franchise Programme (Active — Early Growth)

Hatfield Inns franchise operations are managed through **Hatfield Licensing & Provisions** (see HATFIELD_LICENSING.md for full franchise detail).

**Current state:** ~8-12 franchised Inns open or in build-out, including Savannah, Charleston, Dallas, Dublin, and Napa Valley. Hotels take longer to open than restaurants (higher capital, longer build-out), so the Inn franchise programme has fewer locations than the Bar & Grill franchise but is growing steadily.

The 8 company-owned locations documented in this file remain the quality benchmark. Franchise Inns extend the brand into markets where company-owned hotel investment would be too capital-intensive.

## OPERATIONS

### Staffing Model

Each Hatfield Inn operates with:
- **General Manager** — Full operational authority for the property
- **Front desk team** — 24-hour coverage, bourbon-educated (they can recommend pours and pairings)
- **Housekeeping** — Professional standard. Daily service, quality linens, attention to detail.
- **Lobby bar staff** — Bourbon programme training identical to Bar & Grill. Evening service.
- **Maintenance** — In-house or contracted depending on property size
- **Events coordinator** (larger properties only) — Handles private dining, bourbon tastings, group bookings

### Bourbon Integration

The Inns are a Hatfield brand touchpoint, and bourbon runs through the experience:
- Lobby bourbon bar at every property
- In-room bourbon tasting card (curated selections delivered to room)
- Bourbon education evenings (weekly, casual — a member of staff walks guests through 3-4 expressions)
- Distillery tour packages at Bardstown properties (Inn + tour + Still House dinner)
- Partnership with The Stave: members receive preferred rates at all Hatfield Inns
- No minibar bourbon. This is deliberate — Hatfield doesn't put their product in a minibar next to Pringles. The bourbon experience is hosted, not self-serve.

## NARRATOR RULE — DO NOT EXPLAIN THE INNS IN SCENE

**Hatfield Inns is a hotel group. If it comes up in scene, treat it like any other hotel brand.**

Most likely Hatfield Inns appears when someone else is staying at one, or when it's mentioned in passing. Don't explain the ownership, the concept, or the boutique-bourbon angle. Just name it.

**Wrong:**
> Tommy was staying at the Hatfield Inn on Bree Street — one of the family's boutique hotel properties, mid-tier, bourbon-forward, individually designed for each market.

**Right:**
> Tommy was at the Hatfield Inn on Bree Street.

The context lives here. Not in the prose.
