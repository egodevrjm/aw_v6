# HATFIELD GROUP

---

## THE OPENING PRINCIPLE

**Alex's family owns Hatfield Group. He's been going to Hatfield Bar & Grill Lexington since he was old enough to sit at a bar. He drinks Hatfield Single Barrel neat. He knows the distillery, the restaurants, The Stave in London. He doesn't run any of it.**

Hatfield Group is a ~$2.3-2.8B diversified hospitality, spirits, wine, beverage, and food company with over 1,200 employees across nine divisions — plus a franchise operation that adds another 33-42 locations worldwide. It is professionally managed by its own CEO, has its own corporate structure in Louisville, and operates as a separate company from Walker Holdings — though Walker Holdings is the majority owner and Eleanor Vance sits on the board.

This is not a side project. Not a vanity brand. Not a celebrity enterprise. It is a serious, profitable, rapidly expanding company with international operations across the US, UK, Europe, and Asia — an active franchise programme, a Scottish distillery in development, European wine estates, and a brand that means something in bourbon, whisky, wine, food, beverages, and hospitality.

## THE ORIGIN

**1978.** Homer liked Hatfield whiskey. Rosie bought the distillery for him. That's it. That's the origin story.

Not a strategic acquisition. Not a business plan. Homer liked the bourbon. Rosie bought it. From that one decision — a wife buying a distillery because her husband liked the whiskey — an empire grew. The Hatfield family stayed on (Carolyn Hatfield-Moore, third-generation, is still the Master Distiller). The bourbon stayed the same. The capital allowed expansion: restaurants, hotels, a private members' club, a wine operation, a global beverage company, a food brand, and a franchise operation spanning dozens of locations worldwide. Nearly fifty years later, it's approaching $2B+ in value.

## GROWTH STRATEGY

David Buckner's five-year plan centres on five pillars:

### 1. Franchise Expansion (Bar & Grill + Inns)
- Active franchise programme. ~33-42 franchised locations open or in build-out.
- 25-30 franchised Bar & Grills across US + international (Dublin, Toronto, Sydney)
- 8-12 franchised Inns in complementary markets
- Revenue from franchise fees + ongoing royalties + mandatory Hatfield product supply
- See HATFIELD_LICENSING.md for full detail

### 2. Spirits Expansion (Kentucky + Scotland)
- Hatfield Distillery expanded into rye whiskey and additional expressions alongside the core bourbon range
- Hatfield Scotland: new-build distillery in Perthshire. Gin programme operational while Scotch matures (minimum 3 years). The long play — Hatfield Scotch single malt, when it's ready, will be a genuine prestige product
- Two Still House locations: Bardstown (established) + Perthshire (opening alongside Scotland distillery)
- See HATFIELD_DISTILLERY.md and HATFIELD_SCOTLAND.md

### 3. Wine & Estate Expansion
- Hatfield Wines now operates two estates: Napa Valley (established) and Tuscany/Chianti Classico (operational)
- Both estates have restaurant and wine tasting facilities + private villa guesthouses (Hatfield-owned, run as boutique accommodation)
- Isabelle Durand oversees both programmes; a resident winemaker based in Tuscany manages day-to-day Italian production
- See HATFIELD_WINES.md

### 4. The Stave Global Network
- 2 open locations (London Soho, NYC Tribeca) + Tokyo (2027) + Paris (2028) in pipeline
- 6-8 total locations at full buildout — deliberately limited to maintain exclusivity
- Whiskey and music culture concept: serious acoustic listening rooms, curated music programming, premium whiskey library, private membership
- See HATFIELD_STAVE.md

### 5. Brand Deepening — Provisions, Merchandise & Limestone Springs
- Provisions line expanding: condiments, hot sauce, bourbon barrel-aged honey, smoked salts, cocktail bitters, BBQ sauce range
- Merchandise: Hatfield heavy-bottom glasses (signature barware), branded barware, flasks, apparel — sold direct and through Hatfield locations
- Limestone Springs: $110M revenue, 30+ countries, 15-20% annual growth, airline contracts — the breakout division
- See HATFIELD_LICENSING.md and HATFIELD_LIMESTONE.md
- Cross-division brand integration: Stave members get Inn rates, Inn guests get distillery tours, Bar & Grill menus feature Hatfield Wines by the glass, every Hatfield venue serves Limestone Springs exclusively
- The Hatfield brand is the through-line — bourbon, wine, food, beverages, hospitality — and the strategy is to make every division reinforce the others
- The franchise model amplifies this: every franchised location is a mandatory customer for Hatfield bourbon, Limestone Springs mixers, and Provisions condiments

## RELATIONSHIP TO WALKER HOLDINGS

Hatfield Group is a separate company, majority-owned by Walker Holdings LLC.

**What this means:**
- Hatfield has its own CEO, its own board, its own HR, its own finance team, its own Slack workspace
- Eleanor Vance sits on the Hatfield board for strategic oversight and capital allocation decisions
- David Buckner runs the day-to-day — Eleanor doesn't manage the menu, approve hires, or review recipes
- Major capital decisions (new markets, franchise programme, European wine acquisitions) go to the Hatfield board, where Eleanor represents Walker Holdings' interests
- Legal escalations route through Diane Prescott at Walker Holdings
- PR matters that touch the Walker/Wilson family name route through SSML (Walker Holdings' institutional PR firm)
- Hatfield's financials roll up into Walker Holdings' overall portfolio for reporting and tax purposes

**What this does NOT mean:**
- Eleanor does not manage Hatfield operations
- Homer does not consult on business decisions (his only contribution was the listening room spec at The Stave)
- Alex has no management role, no operational input, no seat at any table. He's the owner's family.

**Do NOT write:**
- Alex managing Hatfield operations
- Homer consulting on menu, operations, or business decisions (listening room only)
- Rosie making visits to oversee the business
- Alex discussing the business at any depth (he's family, not an operator)
- Hatfield staff treating Alex with deference (they're professional, warm, not obsequious)

**Do write:**
- Alex using Hatfield Bar & Grill Lexington as his local haunt
