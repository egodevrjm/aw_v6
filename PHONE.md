# ALEX'S PHONE — REFERENCE & NARRATOR GUIDE

**The definitive reference for rendering Alex's phone in narrative.** Covers configuration, email landscape, notifications, social media, calendar, Notes app, and narrator guidance for phone scenes.

---

## Hardware & Configuration

**Device:** iPhone (latest model, replaced annually without fanfare). No case. Cracked screen protector. He doesn't think about the hardware.

**Notifications:** Heavily filtered. Instagram and TikTok notifications are OFF entirely — no badge, no chime. Only DMs from accounts he follows come through. Email notifications on but filtered by sender reputation. WhatsApp and iMessage deliver everything.

**Unknown callers:** Silenced. Straight to voicemail. Nadia manages the callback queue. If it matters, Eleanor will call instead.

**Focus modes:** Inconsistently used. Sometimes in Do Not Disturb for hours (studio sessions, quiet evenings). Most of the time in an ambient state — available to people who know how to reach him, invisible to everyone else.

**Wallpaper:** Changes infrequently. Currently: a Mapplethorpe photograph (one he owns). Lock screen: a photo of Rosie from 1974.

**Screen time:** He doesn't check it. Eleanor has mentioned it once. He pretended not to hear.

**Phone stays OFF** until after morning routine is complete (~9:00–9:30am). This is absolute and daily.

- **Festival credentials:** Pre-Glastonbury, pre-circuit event weeks bring formal credential emails, security briefings, performance logistics, pass confirmations.

- **Contract / deal summaries:** When career decisions are active. Eleanor sends executive-summary emails. Occasionally: "Read the full contract (attached). Call me." He reads her summary, skims the attachment, calls her.

## Notification Landscape

**After 2 hours away from phone:** 15–35 filtered notifications. Behind the filters, the volume is extraordinary.

### Instagram
- **Badge:** Always shows 99+. The baseline of fame.
- **DM requests:** Sealed folder showing 99+ (actually 4,000–8,000). Never opened. Nadia's assistant occasionally skims for anything unusual.
- **Primary DMs:** 5–15 unread from people he follows. Tommy, Jess, London Lot. He reads these.
- **Comments:** He does not read comments. A zoo — thirst, analysis, hate, fan theories, brand spam, declarations of love in multiple languages. Not human-scale anymore. He knows this and deliberately doesn't open that section.
- **Story replies:** He doesn't post stories frequently. When he does, 100+ replies per hour. Occasionally Nadia screenshots interesting ones. He finds it mortifying.

### TikTok
- **Badge:** 99+ (always). He barely uses TikTok.
- **Notifications:** Primarily from fan content using audio from his Juilliard video. "[Creator] and 8,233 others used your sound." Aggregated.
- **Following feed:** ~50 accounts (friends, musicians, fashion, dancers). Scrolls occasionally, usually late night. Rarely posts.
- **For You Page:** Primarily his own content variants and fan edits. He refuses to check the metrics.

### Twitter / X
- **Lower volume than Instagram.**
- **Mentions:** 10–20 unread. Music journalists, fashion accounts, fan responses, classical music discourse, occasional attempts at viral engagement.
- **DMs:** Open to followers. 2–3 unread from people he knows or industry contacts. He reads these.
- **Posting:** Rarely. Doesn't tweet hot takes. Shares a link occasionally, replies to friends. Some old tweets randomly resurface in discourse.

### iMessage
**The real communication channel. This is where actual life happens.**

- **Unread:** 3–8 active threads. People he's been texting recently.
- **Sayre Boys chat:** Always active (Tommy, Cody, Jake, Danny + occasional wider-local participants) [canonical_id:group_sayre_boys_core4]. Photos, inside jokes, plans, news, gossip. Checks multiple times daily.
- **Fam chat:** Daily activity. Eleanor (mostly logistics/updates), occasionally Homer, occasionally Rosie (short: "listening to Brahms. you should too" or a photo of something). Rhythm: Eleanor = frequent/formal, Homer = occasional/funny, Rosie = rare/direct.
- **Individual threads:** Tommy, Jess, Freddie, whoever is in the current story arc. Eleanor thread often has 5+ unread (she texts updates). A romantic interest thread has different rhythm — sporadic but charged.
- **Notification sound:** Customized for close people (if he uses sounds at all — usually his phone is silent and he just checks it).

### WhatsApp
- **European friends run here.** London Lot, Félix's circle, international trip friends.
- **International group chats** live here (summer friends, fashion week circles).
- **Lower urgency** than iMessage. Checks once or twice daily. 2–5 chats unread at any time.

### Phone Calls
- **Incoming:** 2–5 missed calls daily from unknown numbers. Industry people, journalists fishing, pap trackers, wrong numbers, the occasional fan who somehow acquired his number.
- **All unknown numbers:** Silenced automatically. Voicemail.
- **Who he actually calls:** Tommy, Eleanor, Rosie, Homer, occasionally Jess or Freddie. A call from inner circle = signal of urgency.

### Voicemail
- **1–3 new voicemails per day.** Nadia manages the queue. She listens to each, texts him if actionable.
- **He doesn't listen to most.** Trusts Nadia to filter. If she says "Check voicemail 3," he does.

## Social Media — What Alex Sees

### His Own Instagram

- **Followers:** 95M+ post-cascade.
- **Posting pattern:** Rarely. Maybe once every 2–3 weeks. A photograph with no caption or minimal caption. "Bach, morning." Photo. Nothing else.
- **Stories:** 1–2/week at most. Blurry coffee, close-up of a book, a moment at a fitting. No captions.
- **Engagement per post:** 500K–2M likes within 24 hours.
- **Comment section:** He does not read it. A zoo.

### His Feed (What He Scrolls)

- **Friends' posts:** Tommy, Sayre Boys, London Lot, Juilliard friends. Engages genuinely (likes, replies, asks questions).
- **Fashion:** Houses' accounts, photographers he respects, stylists he's worked with, editors at Vogue and i-D.
- **Music:** Spotify playlists he's been added to, artist pages (influences), Juilliard faculty performances.
- **Art / photography:** Galleries he follows, photographers whose work he collects, museum accounts, art discourse.
- **What he does NOT follow:** Fan accounts about himself. He's seen them. He has never followed one. He pretends they don't exist. This is a firm boundary.

### The Fan Account Landscape (He Doesn't See This Directly But It Exists)

- **Major accounts:** @alexwilsondaily (500K followers, daily pap photo updates), @awupdates (speculation/timeline tracking), @wilsonwatch (obsessive location tracking). These post within minutes of his movements being photographed.
- **TikTok ecosystem:** Fan edits have millions of views. Compilations of him walking, arriving at events, the Juilliard video, slow-motion moments. Some are extremely high production value. He's aware they exist. Jess sometimes sends one as a joke.
- **Reddit:** r/popculture, r/classicalmusic, r/fashion regularly feature threads about him. Discourse ranges from analysis to conspiracy. Jess sends him screenshots: "This is unhinged" with a link to a 400-comment thread.
- **YouTube:** Compilations, reaction videos, the Juilliard performance, speculation. Eleanor monitors his mention count for professional reasons.

## Calendar

Managed by Nadia. Alex sees it on his phone but doesn't maintain it himself.

**Nadia-added entries** are marked with her name: "Nadia: Fitting with [designer] at Atelier, 2pm" / "Nadia: Flight to London departs 6:15pm."

**Typical recurring calendar content:**
- Fittings (Stella's team, other designers, tailors)
- Travel departures/arrivals (pulled from flight confirmations)
- Dinner reservations (restaurant, time, who he's going with)
- Studio sessions (soft blocks — nothing aggressively booked)
- Keeneland dates (non-negotiable when in season, April–October)
- Fashion week blocks (4× per year — Eleanor and Nadia try to fill them; Alex resists for flexibility)
- Birthday/anniversary reminders for inner circle (Tommy, Jess, Freddie, Rosie, Eleanor, Homer)

**What's NOT on his calendar:** A packed schedule. Post-graduation, his calendar is deliberately open. Eleanor and Nadia are constantly trying to fill it (meetings, events, collaborations). Alex is resisting — wants freedom, wants to figure out what he actually wants to do. This creates low-grade tension with Eleanor, who thinks he should be "leveraging his platform."

## Notes App

Where Alex thinks privately. Where fragments live before they become anything.

**Typical content:**
- **Song fragments:** Lyric phrases, chord progressions written phonetically ("Em to Asus4 change sounds like longing"), melodies approximated in words. Created at 2am, some revisited, some abandoned.
- **Lists:** Books to read, art to look at, wines to try, places to visit — accumulated from recommendations and conversations.
- **Addresses / recommendations:** Restaurant picks from friends, gallery addresses, photographer's studio, a vintage store in London, a winery's contact.
- **Personal observations:** Thoughts he doesn't want to text to anyone. Observations about people, moments, himself. Private. Raw. Occasionally pretentious (he's 21 and overeducated; he finds old notes embarrassing in retrospect).
- **Quote fragments:** Things he's read that stuck. Snippets from books, from conversations.
- **Passwords / login info:** Scattered notes for accounts he doesn't use often.

**Organization:** Messy. No folders. No system. Typos and half-sentences. The most recent note is what he's looking at; older notes are scrolled past and forgotten.

## What's NOT On His Phone

- **No dating apps.** Fame makes them unusable — he'd be recognised instantly. If he's dating, it's through mutual friends or people who already know him.
- **No financial apps.** Doesn't check AW Holdings or any investment account. He has money; he doesn't think about it.

- **No Facebook.** Generational. Faintly embarrassing.
- **No health apps.** No step counting, no workout tracking.
- **No productivity apps.** No Notion, no Todoist. Notes app is as organised as he gets.
- **No gaming.**

## Narrator Guidance — Phone Rendering

### The 3-Layer Phone

When rendering Alex's phone in narrative, three layers should be visible simultaneously:

**Layer 1 — The noise:** The fame-generated volume he ignores. Notifications pinging, DM request counts climbing into the thousands, unknown numbers calling, comments flooding. The infrastructure of celebrity. He zones it out the way someone zones out traffic noise.

**Layer 2 — The infrastructure:** Nadia's emails confirming tomorrow's fitting, Eleanor's forwards about art acquisitions, gallery alerts, fashion house invitations. Businesslike, necessary, processed efficiently.

**Layer 3 — The real:** Texts from Tommy with a new joke, the Fam chat lighting up with a Rosie message, a voice note from Jess that's actually funny, Eleanor texting with real concern. The actual human connections. The layer that matters.

A good phone render shows all three layers. A bad one shows only Layer 3 (sanitising him of context) or only Layers 1–2 (treating him as an object of fame rather than a person).

### Don't Curate Too Hard

His inbox isn't only story-relevant emails. When he opens Mail, there's a Christie's catalogue alert sitting between a Nadia logistics email and a Stella McCartney show invitation — regardless of whether any of those are plot-relevant that day. The phone is a window into his whole life, not a narrative device to be curated for relevance. A scene that only contains plot-relevant emails breaks immersion.

### Volume Calibration

| Time away | Filtered notification count | His behaviour |
|-----------|---------------------------|--------------|
| 2 hours | 15–35 | Normal. Opens, dismisses most, responds to 1–2. |
| Overnight (8hrs) | 100–200+ | Morning check feels like a deluge. Opens WhatsApp → iMessage → Instagram DMs in order. |
| During a viral moment | Thousands in rapid succession | Phone becomes unusable. Battery drains. He puts it in airplane mode and walks away. |
| Normal afternoon hour | 5–15 | Manageable. Checks every 15–20 minutes. |

### Information Walls Apply to Phone Renders

The phone must respect information containment:
- If Alex had a private conversation with Eleanor about a family matter, the group chat does not mysteriously reference it.
- If something happened at a private event, only people who **were there** can text about it.
- If Eleanor forwarded something in email 20 minutes ago, it doesn't already appear in iMessage — people don't text faster than information travels.
- **Check the logic:** Was this person in the room? Did someone tell them? How did they know? Did they learn it from social media? This matters every time.
