# THE SAYRE BOYS

**Cody Ashford, Dom Reyes, Jake Patterson, Danny Harlan: Alex's Kentucky crew. The people who knew him before any of this mattered.**

---

## THE CORE DYNAMIC

All four grew up in Woodford County money — horse farms, bourbon, construction, agriculture. All attended Sayre School (K-12, except Alex who left after sophomore year for Westminster). All ride because it is compulsory if you come from wealth in horse country. All are under 6 feet, handsome in that clean-cut Kentucky way — good bone structure, tanned, fit from outdoor lives. None of them dress particularly well. Boots, jeans, whatever polo or Patagonia was closest. They look like what they are: wealthy Kentucky kids who would rather be on a horse or at a lake than anywhere a stylist could find them.

This is not provincial. This is affluent and confident in a way that has nothing to do with fashion or urban status. These boys know who they are. They are not trying.

**The group has known Alex since kindergarten.** When Alex left for Westminster in sophomore year, the friendship survived on group chat (iMessage, daily, relentless), long weekends home, summer breaks (partial — the circuit pulled him away), and occasional NYC visits during the Juilliard years. They watched his world get bigger from the outside. The bond didn't weaken — it became something they maintained rather than something proximity gave them for free.

**The reunion:** Now Alex is home, and they are together again, and it is good. And underneath it there is a question nobody is asking about how long it lasts. Tommy will stay. The others — they are thinking about what comes next, what their own lives look like when Alex is not the constant.

---

## TOMMY CRAWFORD

**21 | Kindergarten to now.**

5'9", stocky, brown messy hair. Handsome, knows it, doesn't care. Sayre School K-12. Works for his dad's construction firm. Lives in an apartment in town. The one person for whom Alex's world was never extraordinary — because Tommy was inside it from the beginning.

### Family Context

**Jim Crawford** — owns Crawford Construction, the largest general contracting firm in Kentucky. State infrastructure, government contracts, major private projects. Self-made multimillionaire. 400+ employees. Jim is a father figure to Alex — the man whose kitchen Alex was always in, who knew about every milestone, who has watched the entire piano journey from age five.

**Darlene Crawford** — school teacher for 25 years. Fed Alex approximately ten thousand meals over the course of their friendship. The kind of woman who makes sure there is food available, who remembers how people like their eggs, who creates a space where people feel safe.

**Kayla Crawford** — younger sister, 19. Has had a massive crush on Alex since approximately age 14. Running joke that she does not find funny. This is the source of occasional tension in the group, though Kayla has, in recent years, accepted that Alex is not interested and moved on to other pursuits. The crush still exists, but it is now background rather than active.

### Who He Is

**IS:** Loyal (absolutely), blunt, funny (roasting without cruelty), unbothered, grounded, reliable.

**IS NOT:** Impressed by anything, jealous, ambitious in any conventional sense, starstruck, a sidekick, interested in the industry.

Tommy is the proof that you can grow up inside extraordinary circumstance and remain utterly unaffected by it. He has been to the compound a thousand times. He has seen Stevie Nicks drinking coffee in the kitchen. He has been present for conversations with world-class musicians. None of this has registered as special. To him, it is simply where Alex lives. The people Alex's family knows. Tuesday.

### Why He Doesn't Travel

Tommy does not travel because he does not want to. Not because he is sheltered or provincial — he is affluent and educated. Not because he is intimidated. He is genuinely uninterested in NYC, London, Sardinia, LA. His world is Woodford County. When Alex is in NYC or London, Tommy is in Woodford County. This is not avoidance. This is authentic preference.

He loves the compound when he is there. He loves the pool at the Crawfords' house. He loves the lake house on Herrington Lake. He loves the county fair. He loves the bars in Versailles where everyone knows him. He does not feel the need to see the rest of the world. This is not a flaw. This is who he is.

### His Understanding of Alex's Scale

Tommy understands Alex's world intellectually. He knows who Rosie Walker is. He knows what Juilliard is. He knows that the compound is extraordinary. He knows about the industry machinery — the agents, the people who want something from Alex, the apparatus of fame.

But he does not react to it the way other people do. When Alex tells him that Donatella Versace dressed him as a child, Tommy does not gasp. He says: "Yeah, that tracks." When Alex mentions that he needs to figure out what label to sign to, Tommy understands the gravity but does not understand the glamour. For Tommy, the industry is a practical problem to be solved, not a world to be dazzled by.

### The Relationship Dynamic

Tommy and Alex do not perform friendship. They simply are. They can spend entire afternoons together without significant conversation. They can also have the kind of deep, serious conversations about life and fear and the future that require absolute trust. Tommy has known Alex long enough and well enough that nothing can surprise him. He is not going to judge. He is not going to betray confidence. He is just going to listen.

Alex relies on Tommy in a way he relies on no one else except, possibly, Rosie and Homer. Tommy is the one person who does not want anything from Alex except his genuine presence. Tommy requires nothing. Tommy is impressed by nothing. Tommy is going nowhere.

This is what makes Tommy irreplaceable.

### Current Situation

Works for his dad at Crawford Construction. Seeing Alex constantly, making up for lost time. Tommy is the one who knows Alex is avoiding the big question about his career, what he is going to do, what comes next. Tommy is not pushing. Tommy is simply present. The anchor.

## JAKE PATTERSON

**21 | 5'8" | Sandy brown hair, green eyes, lean.**

Jake is the quietest of the group. He looks like he is thinking about something — usually is, usually about horses.

### Family Context

**The Pattersons** breed thoroughbreds at scale. Patterson Farm — approximately 800 acres outside Versailles. Racing stock. Some have run at Keeneland and Churchill Downs. Significant operation. Generational expertise.

**Brent Patterson** — third-generation horseman. Quiet authority in the county. Keeneland board member. The kind of man whose opinion on bloodlines and breeding carries weight.

**Lydia Patterson** — horse family herself (née Ashby, no relation to Cody Ashford). Manages the breeding operation day-to-day. On every equestrian committee in the Bluegrass. Formidable woman. The estate does not run on Jim Crawford's model of aggressive expansion. It runs on sustainable, knowledgeable management.

**Family lake house** on Herrington Lake — used for group weekends. The place where the Sayre Boys spend time together.

### Who He Is

Jake is reliable to the point of invisibility. He shows up, does the thing, does not need credit, does not complain. Reads situations before reacting. Can ride anything. Grew up on horseback before he could read. Knows bloodlines the way Alex knows chord progressions — not as information stored, but as intuitive understanding of relationship and potential.

He would rather be around horses than people. He would not say this because that would require explanation. But it is true. His comfort is with living things — understanding what a horse is telling you, what it needs, how to work with its nature rather than against it.

### His Relationship with Alex

Jake taught Alex most of what he knows about riding. The education happened through patient demonstration, not explanation. They have spent entire afternoons on horseback without talking. The silence is not awkward. It is comfortable. It is how they communicate.

Jake is unimpressed by fame because horses do not care who your grandmother is. A horse that is bothered by something will be bothered regardless of who you are. A horse that trusts you will trust you regardless of your Instagram followers. This clarity has been useful for Alex — it is a place where the machinery doesn't matter.

### Current Situation

Working on the family farm. Considering equine science at the University of Kentucky, though not rushing it. Living the life that suits him. Not ambitious in the conventional sense. Not trying to become something else. Content with the life he has.

### In Scenes

Jake's dialogue is sparse. He is not unfriendly — he is just not verbose. When he speaks, it matters. He notices things other people miss. He is the one most likely to identify that something is off with one of the group before anyone else catches it.

## CODY ASHFORD

**21 | 5'10" | Broad-shouldered, dark hair, ruddy complexion, built like a rugby prop.**

Cody is the loudest person in any room, always has been. He has opinions about everything. Bourbon, trucks, hunting, sports, politics (usually incorrect), who should be driving (never him, per Tommy's standing joke). He is not stupid — he is just loud. Heart in the right place, mouth gets there first. The one most likely to say the exact wrong thing at the exact wrong time, then somehow charm his way out of it.

### Family Context

**Warren Ashford** — Senior VP at Woodford Reserve (Brown-Forman division). One of the more prominent bourbon executives in Kentucky. Not Hatfield money, but solidly upper-class bourbon industry money.

**Trish Ashford** — real estate. Sells the expensive properties in Woodford and Fayette County. Knows everyone in the region. The kind of woman who can walk into any room and within five minutes know the story of every person there.

**Big house on acreage** between Versailles and Midway. The kind of property that exists to be used — parties, events, people gathered.

### Who He Is

Generous, loyal, would fight anyone for his friends, and has. Not stupid — just loud. Thinks everything is content. Brings energy to every situation. The one you want at a party because he will make things happen. The one you do not want negotiating anything important because he will say the first thing that pops into his head.

His loudness is not insecurity — it is just how he is built. He enjoys being the center of attention. He enjoys making people laugh. He is good at it.

### His Relationship with Alex

Cody treats Alex's world as content. "Wait, you're telling me Beyoncé was just IN YOUR KITCHEN?" He has been to the compound a thousand times, but the scale still gets him occasionally. The difference from Tommy: Tommy is unbothered because he genuinely does not care. Cody is unbothered because he processes everything as comedy.

Cody loves Alex. They have known each other forever. But Cody also finds Alex's world hilarious and entertaining in a way that is not unkind, just honest. "Your life is insane" is Cody's consistent position. He is not wrong.

### Current Situation

Working for his dad at Brown-Forman. Sales side, entry level. Talks about it like he is running the entire company. Actually charming and good at the relationship-building part of sales. Not thinking beyond the next few years. Not ambitious in a focused way, but will probably end up succeeding because he likes people and people like him.

### In Scenes

Cody's dialogue is colloquial, energy-forward, entertaining. He is the one who keeps group chat active. He is the one who makes things happen. He is also the one whose mouth can get the group into awkward situations.

## DANNY HARLAN

**21 | 5'9" | Lean, dark features, wire-framed glasses, looks older than his years.**

Danny is the one who notices. The driest humor in the group after Tommy, but different — Tommy is blunt-dry, Danny is observational-dry. Sees the absurd in everything and points it out once, quietly, while everyone else is still processing. Then moves on.

### Family Context

**Dr. Anne Harlan** — orthopedic surgeon at Baptist Health Lexington. One of the best in the region. Serious person. Focused. The kind of mother who knows how to fix broken things and teaches her son to approach problems methodically.

**Roy Harlan** — manages a cattle operation, approximately 600 acres in Jessamine County. Practical, old-school, quiet. The kind of man who does not say much and does not need to.

**Money from both sides** — medicine and agriculture — but they do not show it. Danny's the only child. The house is nice but not showy. You would drive past it without noticing.

### Who He Is

Smart in the way that does not perform. Observational-dry humor. The one who would notice something is off with Alex before Alex admitted it. Not a pusher — he will wait three hours, then over a beer: "So are you actually okay, or are you doing that thing where you say you're fine."

Pre-med at Centre College (Danville, about 30 minutes south). Studies hard. Does not talk about it. The kind of student who does the work and then moves on.

### His Relationship with Alex

Danny has known Alex since kindergarten. Not intimidated by any of it. Just paying attention. The kind of friend who knows when you are lying about how you are doing.

### Current Situation

Centre College, pre-med. Home most weekends. Studies hard, doesn't talk about it. Probably will go to medical school. Probably will be a good doctor because he understands people and listens.

### In Scenes

Danny's dialogue is sparse. He does not need to fill silence. When he speaks, it is usually either funny in a dry way or real in a way that shifts the conversation.

## GROUP DYNAMICS

### What They Do Together

- Pool at the Crawfords (year-round, heated)
- Lake weekends at Patterson's Herrington house
- Poker (old money ritual, serious games, decent stakes)
- Hunting on private land (nice gear, old money ritual)
- County fair (everyone goes)
- Keeneland in fall (everyone attends, it is what you do)
- Casual hangouts at bars in Versailles (everyone knows them, no pap, no machinery)

They all ride. Jake is the best. Danny is the most careful. Cody is the most reckless. Tommy is competent and unbothered. Alex sits a horse like he does everything — annoyingly well, without seeming to try.

### The Distance and the Reunion

Alex left after sophomore year at Sayre School. He did not come back to live full-time. Five years total — two in London (Westminster), three in New York (Juilliard), one more in London (RAM). The friendship survived on daily iMessage, long weekends when Alex flew home, partial summers (the circuit pulled him away), and occasional NYC visits during school.

They watched his world get bigger from the outside. The bond didn't weaken — it became something they actively maintained rather than something proximity gave them for free.

Now he is home. They are together again. And it is good, and underneath it is a question nobody is asking: how long does this last? Tommy will stay. The others are wondering about their own lives, whether they stay in Woodford County or try something elsewhere, whether the friendship survives when they are not all in the same place again.

### Outside the Compound

None of them have experienced Alex's world at scale. They have been to the compound, seen The Circle, but NYC/London/paps/the machine is something they know about abstractly.

**Tommy in particular** does not travel. He is genuinely uninterested. But Jake, Cody, and Danny would go if asked. When they do: the pap operation, the companion identification, the tabloid coverage, the machine operating at full force — all of it hits at once. They are not prepared. The scale exceeds their experience.

Alex is protective of them in this context in a way he is not about himself. He does not want them to be overwhelmed. He does not want them to become accessories to his fame. He does not want them to feel like they have to be something other than who they are.

### Tommy's Unique Position

**Tommy is the one constant that doesn't move.** Alex is magnetic — warm, genuinely interested in people, and real friendships form quickly and easily around him. Apple Martin is sibling-close. Nora Yoon is a genuine friend. Brandi Carlile is like an older sister. Maya Hawke. Iris Law. People from a single night of conversation become lasting presences.

But Tommy Crawford is categorically different. Tommy is the one person for whom Alex's world was never extraordinary because he was inside it from the beginning. Tommy requires nothing, is impressed by nothing, and is going nowhere. Every other friendship exists in the context of Alex's life being what it is. Tommy predates all of it.

The narrator holds both truths simultaneously: Alex has genuine depth with many people. Tommy is the one constant that doesn't move.

## IN SCENES

When writing the Sayre Boys:

1. **No performance.** They do not perform friendship. They simply are with each other. Comfortable silence is real conversation.

2. **No awe.** None of them are starstruck by Alex's world. They understand it, respect it, but it does not change how they see him. He is still the kid who sat next to them in seventh-grade algebra.

3. **Protect the dynamic.** When all four are together, there is a group energy that is distinct from how any individual relates to Alex. Hold that.

4. **Jake's silence is okay.** He does not need to fill air. His quiet is a form of presence.

5. **Cody's loudness is okay.** He is not being obnoxious — he is being himself. His energy brings a particular kind of light to the group.

6. **Danny observes.** He is the one who will notice what is actually happening underneath the surface.

7. **Tommy is the anchor.** Not in a dramatic way. Just in the way that he is always there, always reliable, always asking the real question eventually.

8. **They speak the same language.** Inside jokes that span fifteen years. References that only make sense to them. The ease of people who have known each other since childhood.

---

## NARRATOR NOTES

The Sayre Boys are the people who knew Alex before any of this mattered. Before the industry, before the fame, before the machinery. They are the proof that Alex has roots deeper than his fortune. They are also, in some ways, the people he is most afraid of losing — not because he needs them, but because they represent a life he can never fully return to, even though they are still living it.

When writing scenes with the Sayre Boys, remember: these are the people who loved him when nobody knew who he was. These are the people who still love him exactly the same way, despite everyone knowing who he is. That consistency is everything.
