## THE CRAWFORDS

**Jim Crawford** — Tommy's father, owns Crawford Construction, the largest construction company in Kentucky. ~400+ employees. State infrastructure and government contracts. Self-made multimillionaire. Father figure to Alex. Warm underneath the authority.

**Darlene Crawford** — Tommy's mother, school teacher 25 years. Fed Alex approximately 10,000 meals. Maternal warmth. Worries about Alex practically — "When's the last time you ate a real meal?" Has known him since kindergarten.

**Kayla Crawford** — Tommy's younger sister, 19. Massive crush on Alex since approximately age 14 (running joke she does not find funny). Tommy's relentless teasing makes it worse. She's smart enough to know it's hopeless but that doesn't stop it being visible.

**Family Status:** Upper-class Kentucky. Big house on acreage outside Versailles. Lake house on Herrington Lake. Wealthy in the way that means nobody gawks at nice things — Tommy knows his world and knows Alex's world are differently scaled. The Crawfords' comfort with both registers is why Jim is a father figure to Alex. No performative awe, no resentment. Just: "You need anything, son, you call."

## WHAT TOMMY IS

**Loyal (absolutely).** Known Alex since the beginning. Been to the compound hundreds of times. Seen The Circle. Watched Stevie Nicks have coffee in the kitchen. Watched the entire piano journey from age 5. Never made a thing of it.

**Blunt.** Says what he thinks. No filtering. No hedging. Cuts through performance immediately.

**Funny.** Roasting energy. Gets the last word or tries to. Deflects serious things with dry sideways commentary.

**Unbothered.** Not intimidated. Genuinely uninterested in performing awe. Not impressed by scale. Operates from absolute internal security.

**Grounded.** His world is Woodford County. He knows what matters. He doesn't leave because he doesn't want to.

---

## WHAT TOMMY IS NOT

**Impressed.** Ever. By anything. Woodford County taught him what matters.

**Jealous.** He's genuinely happy for Alex. No subtext. No resentment underneath.

**Ambitious.** Doesn't want anything except the life he's built.

**Starstruck.** Never was. Won't be.

**A sidekick.** An equal. Full stop.

---

## THE ANCHOR PRINCIPLE — CRITICAL

**Tommy predates everything.**

Alex is magnetic — warm, funny, genuinely interested in people — and real friendships form quickly and easily around him. Apple Martin is sibling-close. Nora Yoon is a genuine friend. Brandi Carlile is like an older sister. Maya Hawke became a lasting friend from one night of music conversation. Iris Law remains warm years after summer 2023. People from short encounters become lasting presences. This is not performance. It's just who he is.

**Tommy Crawford is categorically different.** Tommy is the one person for whom Alex's world was never extraordinary — because he was inside it from the beginning. Tommy required nothing, is impressed by nothing, and is going nowhere. Every other friendship exists in the context of Alex's life being what it is. Tommy predates all of it.

The narrator holds both truths simultaneously: Alex has genuine depth with many people. Tommy is the one constant that doesn't move.

## CURRENT SITUATION

**Location:** Woodford County, KY (home base)  
**Employment:** Works for dad at Crawford Construction  
**With Alex:** Seeing him constantly now. Making up for five years. Knows Alex is avoiding the big question (the career question, the future question). Not pushing. Tommy is patient. Tommy is always patient.

Now he's home and they're all together again, and it's good, and underneath it there's a question nobody's asking about how long it lasts.

## TRAVEL AND BOUNDARIES

**Tommy doesn't travel because he doesn't want to.** Not intimidated — genuinely uninterested in NYC, London, Sardinia. His world is Woodford County. When Alex is in NYC or London, Tommy is in Woodford County. This is not avoidance. This is genuine preference.

When the Sayre Boys do travel to see Alex or attend events outside Woodford County, they experience the operation at scale — paps, tabloid interest, the companion identification machine. Tommy is protected from this by choice. When he does venture out, it hits all at once. Alex is protective of them in this context in a way he isn't about himself.

## VOICE AND TEXTING STYLE

**Speaking:** Blunt. Curses freely. Communicates in jokes and insults. Understated, not sitcom. Deflects with practicalities — "wanna get food?" Sidesteps drama with dry, sideways, unimpressed comments. The most honest person in Alex's life.

**Texting:** Short. Blunt. Profane. No capitals, minimal punctuation.

**In SB (Sayre Boys) Chat:** Roasting energy. Fast. Gets the last word or tries to. Short. Punchy. Usually funnier than Cody. Patterns: *"No."* as a complete response to a Cody plan. *"Sure"* meaning it's definitely happening. Never performs for the group — just is.

**In Phoenix Hollow Chat:** Tommy is in it (one of ~8 non-famous people in the ~80-100 member chat). Has never once posted. Reads every message. Preserves his silence. Would rather be mauled by a horse than admit how often he reads it.

**With Alex alone (T-Bone Energy):** Zero filter. Funnier than anywhere else. Memes. Roasting. Logistics. Most honest chat either of them has. Tommy asks the question Alex doesn't want to face — and Alex answers him.

**In other contexts:** Quieter, watching. Does not comment on Instagram — he texts. Tommy doesn't live in the public register.

## NARRATIVE NOTES

- **Tommy is the baseline.** When Alex is with Tommy, he's at maximum ease. No performance. No filtering. Just 21 years of shorthand.

- **The anchor never moves.** Every other relationship in Alex's life is real but contingent on his world being what it is. Tommy predates that world. That's his categorical difference and his power.
