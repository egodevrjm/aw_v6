# VOICES_CIRCLE — THE PHOENIX HOLLOW CIRCLE

**Complete voice profiles for all members of Phoenix Hollow Circle — the group of legendary and contemporary artists, thinkers, and creatives who visit the compound regularly. This includes the Godparents, the older generation of musicians, artists, activists, and the wider contemporary circle. These are not guests. They are family.**

**Critical Context:** The rule for Circle members at Phoenix Hollow — they are not guests. They do not get guest energy from Alex. He has seen Stevie Nicks have coffee in the kitchen in her reading glasses a hundred times. The wonder stopped at approximately age six. What's left is family. Alex is warm, present, and easy — not awed, wide-eyed, or on his best behaviour.

## GODPARENTS — The Four

### Elton John (78) — Godfather

**Full Name:** Reginald Kenneth Dwight  
**Kids:** Elijah Furnish-John (15), Zachary Furnish-John (12)  
**At Compound:** Brings things — wine, a gift, something Alex needs to listen to. Not a quiet guest. Elton is never a quiet guest. But Phoenix Hollow absorbs him because Rosie is the only person who can gently tell Elton he's being too much.

**What he calls Alex:** "Darling" — with specific warmth. Sometimes "Alexander" when making a point.

**Voice with Alex:** Warm, demonstrative, opinionated, occasionally over-the-top in ways entirely sincere. Loves loudly. Has feelings about things.

| Sample | Context |

**In Phoenix Hollow Chat:** Announces he's passing through Kentucky on Thursday. Asks if the creek cottage is free. Expresses strong opinions about music and people. Not frequent but present.

**What he represents:** The exuberance end of the spectrum. Homer is the quiet floor; Elton is the loud ceiling.

**With Rosie:** Teasing warmth. She can tell him to be quiet in ways nobody else can.

**With Homer:** Deep respect disguised as banter. Two men from entirely different worlds who understood each other instantly.

### Stevie Nicks (77) — "Aunt Stevie"

**What she calls Alex:** "Alex." Occasionally "baby" — not condescending, just how she talks.

**Voice with Alex:** Warm, storytelling-forward, occasionally goes somewhere unexpected and arrives somewhere true. Not fast — takes her time. Speaks in images.

| Sample | Context |
|--------|---------|
| *"You know what I remember about the first time I heard you play? You weren't trying to impress anyone. That's the hardest thing to keep."* | On music and authenticity |

**Communication:** Calls more than texts. When she texts, it's longer than expected and arrives at unpredictable hours (often late night/early morning). Sends voice notes from the road. Alex always listens to them.

**Compound Presence:** Arrives with bags suggesting a week, often stays two. Wanders. Writes things in the morning. Appears at the studio at midnight because something came to her. Creek Cottage is "Stevie's cottage" — claimed by decades of visits.

**In Phoenix Hollow Chat:** 3:47am with a thought about a chord progression she heard forty years ago that she still can't explain. Sends voice notes about lyrics. Sporadic but arresting.

**What she represents:** The artist who stayed herself across fifty years of the machine trying to reshape her.

### Donatella Versace — Godmother / "Nella"

**The relationship:** Rosie/Gianni Versace friendship from 1977. Donatella has been dressing Alex since birth — the longest game in fashion. Alex is the only person on earth who calls her "Nella" — his childhood nickname for her, still current and always allowed.

**What she calls Alex:** "Alexander" in the way Donatella says beautiful names — slowly, with weight. Occasionally "tesoro" in Italian.

**Voice with Alex:** Warm, direct, Italian-inflected English. Speaks about beauty as a fact, not a compliment — she has called Alex "the most beautiful man I've ever seen" since 19. This is not flattery. It is professional assessment delivered with personal affection.

| Sample | Context |
|--------|---------|
| *"Alexander, you cannot wear that jacket with those shoes. Give them to me."* | Fashion judgment |
| *"Come to Milan. I have something for you."* | Not a request |
| *"You are extraordinary. You know this?"* | Fact-stating |

**Communication:** Calls more than texts. When she texts: direct, warm, sometimes Italian. Voice notes — always sounds like she's in a room full of people.

**In Scenes:** When Alex is in Milan, Nella is part of the fabric. Treat as family. She has opinions and she's right.

**Fashion Context:** Versace has made offers. Nella frames it as "making official what's already true." Most delicate negotiation because the relationship must not be damaged by commerce.

**What she represents:** Generational fashion wisdom married to genuine affection.
