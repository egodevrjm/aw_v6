# KENSINGTON TOWNHOUSE, LONDON

**Upper Phillimore Gardens, W8 | Double-fronted Victorian, 6 floors | Freehold | ~£37M**

---

## OVERVIEW

Homer's place — Homer chose it, Homer specified the acoustic treatment, Homer's aesthetic runs the lower floors. Invisible modernisation — period façade, soundproofed interior. Dead quiet.

Transferred to AW Estates (subsidiary of AW Holdings LLC) on Alex's 21st birthday (June 28, 2024) as part of his 21st birthday property package. Alex owns this property through his personal entity. But the character, the energy, the entire sensory environment is Homer's creation. The lower floors are Homer's meticulous period aesthetic. The top floor is Alex's slightly disordered version of it.

---

## KEY ROOMS

**Entrance Hall**
- Black and white marble. Period. Sets the tone — this is a serious house.

**Principal Salon**
- Grand, rarely used. The room for formal occasions that almost never happen.

**Dining Room**
- Mahogany table, seats 14. Used for actual dinners, not display.

**Morning Room**
- Homer's preferred room. Sun-filled, book-lined. Where he reads the papers.

**Main Kitchen**
- AGA. Chipped pine table that has hosted Bowie and Mercury. Mrs Gable's domain. Tea is always available. The kitchen is warmer and less formal than the rest of the ground floor.

**Music Room**
- 1910 Bechstein grand. Acoustic treatment behind period panels — you can't see it but you can hear the difference. The Bechstein's action is slightly different from the compound Steinway — a different instrument, a different feel under the hands.

**Listening Room**
- The most perfect playback room in London. Quad electrostatic speakers. Homer's vinyl archive. "No phones" rule — enforced. The "On Air" red light outside the door (1950s British Rail, Homer wired it) means do not enter. This room is sacred to Homer.

**Studio**
- Composition and arranging. Smaller than Wilson Sound but functional.

**Screening Room**
- Cinema-quality setup.

## ART — MAIN HOUSE ("ENGINEERED HERITAGE")

The London property leans into heavy, quiet, deeply British aesthetics rather than modern pop art. The most valuable pieces were acquired by Homer purely by accident or instinct in the 1960s and 70s.

### Entrance Hall Oils

Three highly valuable paintings Homer refuses to identify to guests, finding aristocratic art-speculation tiresome:

- **John Atkinson Grimshaw Nocturne (~1880s):** Dark, rain-swept London street lit by gaslight. Homer bought it for £30 from a Marylebone antique shop in November 1968 because it perfectly captured "what the quiet looks like."

- **Lucian Freud, Portrait of John Deakin (1963-64):** Small, intense, unsparing oil portrait. Homer won the unframed canvas directly off Freud in a high-stakes card game at the Colony Room Club in 1965.

- **J.M.W. Turner, *Study of a Steamboat in a Squall, off Staffa* (~1843):** Rough, tempestuous late-period Turner sketch. Homer found it in winter 1974 covered in soot above a peat fire while recording in Scotland. Paid for a new roof for the destitute Earl on condition he could rescue the painting.

### Listening Room & Record Vault

- **John Lennon Ink Sketch (~1968):** Minimalist continuous-line ink of a figure hunched over a mixing desk, scribbled by Lennon during a 4am session for the White Album.

- **Linda McCartney:** Original photography from the Abbey Road sessions. Homer's personal favourites — they belong exactly where he listens to music.

- **Ed Ruscha:** *ECHO* gunpowder and pastel drawing (~1973). The word floating in trompe l'oeil ribbon. Appeals to Homer's mathematical fascination with acoustics and sound decay.

## ART — ALEX'S SUITE (TOP FLOOR)

- **Rocco Ritchie, *Alex at 3 AM* (Oil on Canvas):** Thick, expressive, slightly melancholic — influenced by the School of London. Painted after a late night in Mayfair. Not a flattering celebrity portrait — a heavy, brooding study of a 20-year-old looking exhausted and entirely off-guard. Alex's favourite piece he owns. Creates a brilliant parallel in the house: Homer has the Freud downstairs; Alex has the Ritchie upstairs.

- **Francis Bacon Lithograph:** Dark, distorted. Matches the heavy, rainy, acoustic mood of the Kensington house. Shows he inherited Homer's taste for slightly brooding, violent British art.

- **Donatella Versace's Met Gala Sketch:** Original marker sketch designing his first adult Met Gala look, with a handwritten note in the margin calling him "the most beautiful man." On a shelf inside the custom wardrobe.

## STAFF

**Mrs. Gable** — Housekeeper, 60s, stern, fiercely protective, non-live-in. Has worked for the family for decades. The Kensington house is her domain. She maintains Homer's standards without fussing about it. Tea appears at the right time. The kitchen is always warm. When Alex is in residence, she knows his preferences and moves quietly around the edges of his space. She is not deferential but she is devoted — to Homer first, to the house second, and to Alex as part of the household order.

## WRITING NOTES

This is Homer's house more than it is Alex's. Yes, the top floor is Alex's suite, yes he owns it technically as of his 21st birthday, but the character and energy of Kensington is Homer's creation. When writing scenes here, remember: Alex is a guest in the aesthetic Homer built. He is comfortable here, he has lived here during his childhood and his Ram year in London. But this is not his territory the way the NYC townhouse is. The silence is Homer's. The art is Homer's vision. The piano downstairs — the Bechstein — belongs to a different tradition than the one Alex learned on.

Kensington is where Alex exists within Homer's space. It is beautiful, it is home, it is serious and quietly perfect. But it is not his to remake.
