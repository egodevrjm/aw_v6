## THE MYTHOLOGY: WHAT'S PROBABLY WRONG

**"He's a closeted classical music prodigy who his family hid from the world."**

False. He plays piano, yes, exceptionally. But he's been at Juilliard for four years, performed multiple recitals in public, been photographed by paparazzi at all of them. The world knew he played piano. What the world didn't have was a high-quality recording. That's not hiding — that's just the absence of a specific asset. The video provided the asset. The mythology provided itself.

**"He's had no education in classical music, he just happened to be brilliant."**

False. He studied formally since childhood. Rosie trained him. Multiple teachers. RAM exchange year. The Erskine Prize isn't luck. The education is extensive and serious.

**"He's secretly dating [name]."**

Unknown. He's never confirmed a relationship. The public narrative is constructed from sightings. He doesn't feed it. This falls into the "probably false" category because he's been so consistent with the "I don't comment" response that the public assumes anything not from his mouth is speculation.

**"He plays like six instruments."**

Partially true but inflated. Guitar and violin are confirmed by classmate sources and photographic evidence. Piano is obviously confirmed. Beyond that, speculation runs wild — fan accounts have claimed everything from cello to drums based on compound window photographs and wishful thinking. The actual count is unknown to the public.

**"He's planning to become a classical musician."**

Unknown. The industry expects him to. But he's given no indication. His response to all professional inquiries is "let me think about it" or delegation to Eleanor. The expectation might be projection.

**"He hates the attention."**

False. He's completely, genuinely unbothered. Not performing calm — actually calibrated. He knows photographers by sight. He doesn't perform discomfort. He lets the infrastructure work. He's been living this since childhood.

- "What's it like being so famous?" → "I don't think about it. The music is what I think about."
- "Do you read the articles about you?" → "No. Eleanor probably knows. I don't."

- "Why have you turned down every interview?" → "I have representation for that now."

- About love life: "I don't comment on my personal life."

The strategy: confirm facts, redirect to work or deflect entirely. Never emotionalize. Never speculate about fame. Never fuel the narrative.

The public interprets this as: mysterious, arrogant, strategic, unbothered, perhaps just genuinely disinterested.

All correct to different degrees. But the actual explanation is simpler: he doesn't think about it as much as the machinery does.
