# LEGACY SUMMER

**The European summer social world: Ibiza, Mykonos, Capri, Monaco, Costa Smeralda. The recurring cast Alex encounters on the summer circuit.**

---

## WHAT "LEGACY SUMMER" MEANS

The "Legacy" in Legacy Summer refers to the previous generation — Rosie and Homer's generation — having established these connections. Rosie and Homer traveled to Europe regularly starting in the 1970s and 1980s. They built relationships with people, with places, with the rhythm of the European summer. Alex inherited access to this world from them.

He did not have to earn his way into these spaces. He was born into them. The villas are available to him. The people know who he is. The infrastructure exists because Rosie and Homer built it decades ago.

But what Alex has done is make this world his own. The friendships he has formed in the summer circuit are real, not derived from his parents. The places he goes back to have become his places. The summer rhythm — Glastonbury in late June, Sardinia in July, Ibiza for a stretch, back through London or to LA — has become his own circuit.

---

## VILLA SERENA — THE ANCHOR

**Costa Smeralda, Sardinia | Private cove | Two weeks every July | Non-negotiable.**

Villa Serena is the anchor point of Alex's summer. It is a private villa with private beach access — a cove that is inaccessible by foot from public beaches. No photographers can reach it. No paps can stake it out. It is one of the few places where Alex can be truly private.

The villa belongs to the family's holdings (part of the Walker Properties portfolio). It sits on the Costa Smeralda in Porto Cervo, surrounded by other significant properties but isolated by geography and access.

**What happens at Villa Serena:**

Alex arrives in mid-July. Usually stays two weeks. The routine is unhurried: swimming in the morning (the sea is extraordinary), time to think, conversations with whoever is there, boat trips to nearby islands. There is no agenda. Things happen organically.

People flow in and out: La Terrazza friends (Tommaso, Edo, Camille, Lucía, Félix), occasional other guests, sometimes nobody for long stretches. The villa has staff who handle logistics, but the experience is one of complete freedom.

This is the most relaxed Alex is all year. Not because he is hiding — because there is nothing to perform for. The sea is there. The people are good. The time is his.

## LA TERRAZZA CORE

**La Terrazza — "The Terrace." Alex's European social circle, anchored in Milan and Sardinia. The equivalent of the London Lot or New York Lot but for the Mediterranean world.**

This group is not a summer-only phenomenon. These are people who know each other year-round — Milan, Paris, Rome, Madrid, the broader European social infrastructure. They happen to converge most visibly in Sardinia in July, but the friendships, the WhatsApp chat, the dinners, the connections operate across the entire year. Tommaso is in Milan. Camille is in Paris. Lucía is in Madrid. They see each other at fashion weeks, at gallery openings, at dinners in cities across Europe. Sardinia is where the group is most fully assembled. It is not the only place the group exists.

These are people who grew up coming to the Costa Smeralda. Not discovering it as adults. Coming to it as children, learning the routes, learning the rhythm, learning that Sardinia is the place you return to every summer.

**Language:** The group operates across Italian, French, Spanish, and English — often within the same conversation. Alex's four languages are not decorative here; they are functional. Tommaso switches between Italian and English mid-sentence. Camille defaults to French when she is thinking. Lucía's Spanish breaks through when she is emphatic. Félix moves between French and English depending on who he is addressing. The chat runs the same way — multilingual, code-switching, nobody translates for anyone because nobody needs to. English is the default lingua franca when the full group is together, but it is not the only language in the room.

**Chat:** LT — La Terrazza (WhatsApp, ~8-10). See VOICES_CHATS.md for full chat rendering rules.

### TOMMASO CORSINI

**24 | Milan / Sardinia | La Terrazza anchor | Italian industrial family.**

Tommaso's family has had a villa in Sardinia for thirty years. He has been coming here since he was a child. Porto Cervo is his summer home in the truest sense.

**Who he is:** Intelligent, connected, warm. He moves through multiple worlds simultaneously — Milan art/design, the Sardinian summer circuit, the broader European social infrastructure. He is not performing this. He is simply someone whose life encompasses multiple places and multiple communities.

**His role:** Tommaso is the one who knows the rhythm of the summer. He knows which restaurants are good this year. He knows which boat captains are reliable. He knows how to make things happen. He is the person who organizes boat trips, who suggests dinner reservations, who brings people together.

**La Terrazza significance:** Tommaso's family connection to La Terrazza (the restaurant that functions as the social hub) makes him the natural anchor of the group. He was practically raised there. He is known by the staff. He brings people in.

**Relationship to Alex:** Genuine friendship. Tommaso is older (24 to Alex's 21) but the dynamic is peer, not mentorship. They have chemistry as friends. Tommaso understands Alex's world and does not make a thing of it. Alex understands that Tommaso's world (industrial Milan money, the European social infrastructure) is its own realm of significance.

### EDO FERRETTI

**25 | Rome / Ibiza / everywhere | DJ, promoter, chaos anchor.**

Edo is the person for whom the summer circuit is not quite geography — it is more like a series of locations that are simply where the party is. Based officially in Rome. Spends significant time in Ibiza. Shows up in Sardinia. Materializes wherever his network is active.

**Who he is:** Edo knows the music world. He is a DJ and promoter who understands the electronic music infrastructure — the DJs, the producers, the venues, the boat captains, the villa fixers. He is connected at a level that transcends normal social networking.

**Relationship to Alex:** Genuine friendship grounded in shared understanding of what the summer circuit is. Edo does not worship Alex's world. He offers Alex access to his world — the music, the venues, the people, the after-parties where the actual good music is happening at 4am.

## PAP DYNAMICS ON THE CIRCUIT

### SARDINIA (VILLA SERENA)

The private cove is genuinely inaccessible — photographers cannot reach it by foot or road from public beach. Boat access requires knowing where to anchor. Exposure: very low when Alex is at the villa.

**Via del Porto (Porto Cervo main shopping/social area):** Has photographers. Transit through Porto Cervo is photographed. Private boat transfers reduce exposure — if you're arriving from a private dock to a private boat, the window is short.

**Overall:** Low pap presence on villa grounds, medium presence in town. The geography helps significantly.

### IBIZA

**Town (Ibiza Town and Sant Antoni):** Has photographers. Clubs are dark inside. Boat days are private. Villa hills above coast are difficult terrain. Exposure at arrival/departure and at the few public-facing beach clubs. Medium pap situation overall.

### GLASTONBURY

**Crowd absorption:** 200,000 people in a field. The scale of the crowd disperses the usual pack dynamic. Telephoto lens photography is possible but coordinated stalking is difficult. Fan account tracking exists but festival chaos absorbs it. Medium pap situation overall — documented less than formal events, more than private locations.

### LA / MALIBU / HOLLYWOOD BOWL

**LA paps work differently** — they drive rather than cluster. Chateau Marmont has its own relationship with photographers. San Vicente Bungalows has strict no-photography policies. Malibu beach is genuinely photographable from water if timing is right. Medium-High pap situation overall.

### HAMPTONS

**August peak:** Well-organized paparazzi infrastructure. Main Street East Hampton has cameras. Beach at Gin Lane gets boat photographers. Private estate parties are genuinely private. Visibility-to-privacy ratio worse than Sardinia, better than Manhattan.

## WHAT THE SUMMER MEANS

This is Alex's first completely free summer since age four. For the first time, he is not returning to school in September. For the first time, there is no institutional deadline or return date.

The summer is his to structure. Rosie and Homer have no expectation that he will be home all summer — they have always understood that the summer circuit is how Alex's world works. But they also expect him home for significant stretches.

The tension between the pull of the summer circuit (Sardinia, the friends, the ease) and the responsibility of being home in Woodford County will be an ongoing negotiation.

The summer is also when the career question that has been held at bay becomes active. Eleanor has calls to return. The industry has been waiting for the moment Alex graduated. The summer is when real conversations start happening about what comes next.

## THE LA TERRAZZA GROUP IS NOT THE ALEX CHANNEL

The same principle as every other social circle, in the European register:

1. **The group has its own life.** Tommaso and Edo had dinner in Milan last Tuesday. Camille went to an opening in Paris. Lucía is in Madrid arguing about politics. Félix is at a vineyard. None of this involved Alex. The WhatsApp chat was active while he was asleep in Kentucky. When he opens it, there are conversations in Italian he needs to scroll back through, a restaurant recommendation from Camille he missed, a joke between Edo and Tommaso he doesn't get because he wasn't there Wednesday.

2. **Vary the cast.** The named profiles are the core. They are not the only people at the table. The La Terrazza world includes European royalty, old money, fashion people, art people, minor aristocracy, friends-of-friends who've been coming to Sardinia for years. When rendering a Sardinia dinner, the table should include people who aren't in this file — someone's cousin from Florence, a girl Camille brought from Paris, a Spanish architect Lucía knows.

3. **Generate voices in multiple languages.** Not every message in the LT chat is in English. Italian is the default when Tommaso and Edo are talking. French when Camille drives. Generate messages in the original language with enough context that the reader follows — Alex does, because he speaks all of them.

4. **Alex is not always present and the group doesn't pause for him.** Especially in the off-season (October-April), the group is having dinners and conversations across Europe. Alex is in Kentucky. The friendship survives this because it's real, not because it's Alex-dependent.

## NARRATOR NOTES

1. **The summer circuit is organic, not Alex-centric.** These places and these people exist whether Alex shows up or not. He is a node in existing ecosystems, not the center around which they organize.

2. **Villa Serena is genuinely private.** It is one of the few places where Alex can be completely off the grid. Protect that in scenes.

3. **La Terrazza and the group around it are genuinely important.** These friendships are real. The people care about Alex, not about his fame or his access.

4. **The summer has different registers in different locations.** Sardinia (private, easy, intimate), Ibiza (music-forward, energy high, less personally reflective), Glastonbury (crowd, music, community, public-facing), LA (industry-adjacent, career conversations, different atmosphere).

5. **Apple Martin matters in the summer context.** She makes things easier for Alex. Her presence normalizes the space in a way that allows genuine relaxation.

6. **The chat exists to make logistics possible.** The Summer House chat is practical and social simultaneously — it is how the group coordinates but also how friendships maintain between physical locations.

7. **This is the first summer where Alex is not obligated to return to school.** That freedom is new and significant. It changes the calculus of how he structures his time.

8. **La Terrazza is year-round, not summer-only.** The group converges most fully in Sardinia in July. But these are real friends who see each other in Milan, Paris, Madrid, Rome throughout the year. The chat runs all year. The friendships don't hibernate.
