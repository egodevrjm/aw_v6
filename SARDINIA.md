# VILLA SERENA, SARDINIA

**Costa Smeralda, Sardinia | Private cove & headland | ~$70M**

---

## OVERVIEW

Transferred to AW Estates (subsidiary of AW Holdings LLC) on Alex's 21st birthday (June 28, 2024) as part of his birthday property package. The family's Mediterranean property. Homer acquired the original farmhouse and headland plot in 1986 — four hectares of pink granite above a private cove, twelve minutes by road from Porto Cervo. The land was purchased from a Genoese family who had held it for three generations and never developed it beyond a summer cottage.

Homer spent the next twenty years building it into what it is now. Not all at once — the compound grew the way the coast itself was developed, one structure at a time, each one integrated into the rock. The main villa came first. Then the pavilions. The boathouse in the mid-nineties, the spa and gym complex in 2004, the padel court added in 2018. It reads as a single coherent estate because each addition was designed by the same Milanese architect — a Vietti student who understood that the point was never to impose on the landscape but to grow out of it.

One of the finest private estates on Costa Smeralda. Not many people know it exists. That is intentional.

**White stone and terracotta. Five guest pavilions stepped into the hillside. A private cove. The sea.**

**Virtually no paparazzi presence on the grounds or the private cove. The most relaxed Alex is, publicly, all year.** He has been coming every July since he was old enough to travel. He has no complicated feelings about it. It's where they go. It's summer.

## PROPERTY LAYOUT

The estate occupies a granite headland above a private cove, approximately four hectares in total. The architecture is consistent throughout — pale dressed stone, terracotta tile, carved arches, Mediterranean timber. Everything sits low against the rock. From the water, the rooflines disappear into the scrubland. From the terraces, the sea is everywhere.

The compound is structured as a main villa flanked by five guest pavilions, with the service buildings, spa complex, and boathouse lower on the slope, connected by stone paths and covered walkways that weave between granite outcrops and mature carob trees.

---

### THE MAIN VILLA

The heart of the estate. Three floors built into the hillside, the lowest level opening directly to the upper terrace and pool, the uppermost level containing Alex's master suite.

**Alex's Master Suite** — the entire top floor of the main villa. Bedroom, sitting room, private terrace cantilevered above the cove. The terrace is the best seat on the property: unobstructed sea to the horizon, the private cove below, the islands offshore. Dressing room, marble bathroom with freestanding bath and outdoor shower on the terrace.

**Main Living Spaces** — ground and first floor. The main drawing room runs east-west, floor-to-ceiling glass doors folding back to dissolve the distinction between inside and outside. A library and music room (upright piano, not a concert instrument — Alex plays it anyway). A formal dining room that seats twenty. An informal dining terrace adjacent to the kitchen that is used for every meal that doesn't require the formal room, which is most of them.

**The Kitchen** — fully professional. The seasonal chef works here. Large central island, open shelving, a pass-through to the terrace. The kitchen is excellent but not showy. It exists to produce good food, not to be photographed.

### THE GUEST PAVILIONS

Five freestanding pavilions arranged in a loose arc across the upper hillside, each connected to the main villa by covered stone walkways. Each pavilion is architecturally identical in language but oriented differently to the light and the view. Each has its own private terrace, sitting room, two bedrooms (a principal room and a companion/guest room), and two bathrooms.

| Pavilion | Orientation | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Pavilion One** | Due south, faces the open sea | Farthest from main house — most private. Reserved for VIPs or family. |
| **Pavilion Two** | Southeast, cove view | Closest to the main house. Often used by Kevin, heads of security, or Alex's PA when resident. |
| **Pavilion Three** | South-southwest, partial sea and garden | Quiet, shaded in the afternoon. Preferred by guests who want to sleep late. |
| **Pavilion Four** | Southwest, hillside garden | Most enclosed. Overlooks the padel court and garden terrace rather than the sea directly. |
| **Pavilion Five** | South, upper terrace and infinity pool | Direct access to the main pool terrace. Used by whoever Alex wants close. |

Each pavilion sleeps 2–4 depending on configuration. Total guest capacity across all five: up to 20 guests at full occupancy.

Used for extended staff in Alex's entourage who travel as guests rather than employees, or for overflow when all five pavilions are occupied.

### THE TERRACES & POOLS

Multiple levels of terracing cascade from the main villa down toward the cove.

**The Main Terrace & Infinity Pool** — directly off the main living floor. The primary social space. Long salt-water infinity pool extending to the southern edge, where it appears to meet the sea 40 metres below. Sunbeds, a bar cabinet, a dining table for sixteen under a pergola. Morning coffee here. Evening aperitivos here. Every meal that isn't a formal dinner happens on this terrace.

**The Lower Terrace** — midpoint of the slope, between the main terrace and the cove. A second, smaller plunge pool. More private, more shaded. Where people actually swim rather than be seen swimming.

**The Cove Terrace** — a stone platform at water level, directly above the private cove. Steps cut into the granite leading down to the water. This is where the boats are accessed, where people swim, where the day starts.

---

### THE PRIVATE COVE

The headland shelters a small private cove — roughly 60 metres of pinkish-white sand, granite boulders on both arms, clear turquoise shallower water fading to deep Mediterranean blue. Not accessible from the public coastal path. Entry is either from the estate's own cove steps or by water.

Permanent exclusion buoys mark the cove boundary. Security monitors the water perimeter during Alex's residency. Drone access is restricted under Italian maritime airspace rules. Effectively invisible from any public vantage point.

The water is extraordinary. That is not negotiable. It is the point of everything.

---

### THE BOATHOUSE & DOCK

Purpose-built boathouse on the northern arm of the cove, integrated into the granite. Stone construction with tiled roof, matching the estate architecture.

**Vessels berthed (full summer complement):**
- One 42-foot day cruiser (the primary boat, used for coastal runs, offshore swimming, restaurant transfers)
- One 22-foot RIB tender (for beach transfers, water sports, supply runs)
- Two sea kayaks, paddle boards, snorkelling equipment

**Boathouse infrastructure:** Fresh water wash-down, fuel tank, marine electrical, tool and maintenance storage, a small crew room above for the two marine staff who run the dock.

The dock extends into the cove from the boathouse arm. Guests board here. Supplies arrive here when delivered by water. Porto Cervo Marina is twelve minutes by water, less by fast RIB.

---

### SPA & GYM COMPLEX

A freestanding stone building on the lower hillside, between Pavilion Four and the boathouse path. Purpose-built during the 2004 expansion. Architecture matches the estate: same stone, same tile, low roofline, integrated with the surrounding carob and mastic trees.

**Gym:** Professional equipment — Technogym, free weights, cable stations. Natural light through east-facing arched windows. A/C. Used by Alex most mornings, and by security and entourage throughout the day.

**Spa:** Two treatment rooms (masseuse travels with the entourage in July; a local therapist is on a standing weekly call as backup). Steam room. Cold plunge. Outdoor shower on a private stone terrace surrounded by mature vegetation.

**Changing Facilities:** Shared between the spa and the gym, but separated by the central corridor.

---

### PADEL COURT

Added 2018. Single padel court on the northern slope of the estate, set into a levelled granite platform with glass side walls. Cushioned surfaces, lighting for evening play. A viewing bench with a sea sightline. Nadia arranges equipment and can source a hitting partner on request through her local network. Porto Cervo has a padel culture — there are players available.

## STAFF & OPERATIONS

**Nadia** — Local property and household manager. Not exclusive to the Walkers — she manages three estates along this stretch of coast for comparable families. This is how Costa Smeralda works at this tier: a trusted local network, the same people year after year, shared between the houses that matter. Nadia has been managing Villa Serena since 1997. She knows the property better than anyone alive. She is not emotionally attached to the family in the way Woodford or London staff are — she is a skilled professional who takes her work seriously and charges accordingly. She handles: property maintenance and year-round oversight, seasonal opening and closing, seasonal staff recruitment, provisioning and supplies, dock coordination, local vendor relationships.

**Seasonal Chef** — Brought in for July. Local, well-trained, market-informed. Meals are excellent and entirely simple — grilled fish, local vegetables, good bread, aged cheese, fruit. Not a tasting-menu chef. A summer food chef, which is a different and arguably more useful thing.

**Marine Staff (×2)** — Two crew, employed directly for the July season. They live in the boathouse crew room. They manage the boats, do the transfers, run provisioning runs by water to Porto Cervo, and keep the dock operational. One is a certified diver.

**Housekeeping (×3)** — Seasonal, recruited by Nadia. Daily service to the main villa and all occupied pavilions.

**Security** — Alex travels with his own security team. During Villa Serena residency, two security staff are accommodated in Pavilion Two or the overflow rooms depending on entourage size. They assess the cove perimeter and coordinate with the estate gate on arrivals and departures. The estate's own gate (electronic, camera-monitored) is managed by a local security firm contracted through Nadia.

**Year-Round Maintenance** — Coordinated through Nadia. A team of four local tradespeople on retainer: electrician, plumber, stone mason, gardener. The gardens are tended year-round; the estate is opened formally three weeks before Alex's arrival every July.

## THE SUMMER RHYTHM

July is the rhythm. Mornings in the water. Late breakfasts on the main terrace. Afternoons quiet or with guests — the London Lot sometimes, Lila and Kaia, whichever friends are on the Mediterranean circuit. Late dinners that don't start until 10pm. The sound of the boats at the dock. Italian spoken as naturally as English. The weight of heat and light. Nobody performing. Everyone in summer clothes and bare feet.

The nearby Porto Cervo infrastructure is there if needed — dinner at I Frati Rossi, a night at Phi Beach, shopping in the Piazzetta — but Villa Serena is retreat, not circuit. The point is that nothing leaks. The paparazzi photos from Sardinia are genuine: spontaneous, private, the family doing nothing. It is the only place where Alex has total public relaxation.

When people arrive by boat rather than road — which is encouraged — they see the property from the water first. The pale stone compound on the headland, terraces stacked into the hillside, the infinity pool's edge hanging against the sea. It is one of the most beautiful private properties on the Smeralda coast. Homer built it to last. It will outlast all of them.

## WRITING NOTES

Villa Serena is fundamentally different from the other properties. It is not a family seat (that is Woodford). It is not a creative headquarters (that is London). It is not a property Alex chose for himself (that is New York or LA). It is a summer place that Homer built, that became the family's annual ritual, and that Alex inherited with twenty years of childhood memory already baked into it.

When writing scenes at Villa Serena: the pace is slower. The architecture is the best version of what the coast can offer, but that is not what anyone is thinking about. Alex's familiarity with the place is complete and effortless — he doesn't navigate it consciously, he moves through it the way you move through anywhere you've known since childhood.

The scale of the estate — five pavilions, the boathouse, the spa, the cove — makes it capable of hosting a full entourage without anyone being in anyone else's way. That matters. It means Villa Serena can be the setting for genuine solitude (Alex on the cove terrace at 6am, nobody else awake) and for a full social summer (twelve guests across the pavilions, late dinners, boats, daytrips to Porto Cervo). The property accommodates both registers without contradiction.

This is the one place where being internationally famous creates no friction at all. The paparazzi may exist somewhere in the world. They do not exist here.
