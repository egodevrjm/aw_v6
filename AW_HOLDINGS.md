## STRUCTURE

AW Holdings LLC is a subsidiary of Walker Holdings, established by Eleanor Vance on the day Alex was born. It exists to hold, manage, and grow Alex's personal inherited wealth separately from both the Walker Holdings parent entity and Alex's personal Walker trust distributions. When Alex eventually inherits Walker Holdings in full, AW Holdings will fold back into the parent structure — but until then, it operates as a ring-fenced entity with its own balance sheet.

Eleanor oversees the entity at board level, but as of 2024, AW Holdings operates as a proper standalone organization with dedicated staff, its own governance, and professional investment management. Alex has full access but exercises it rarely and only for considered purchases — art, vintage fashion, first editions, collectables. He does not treat it as spending money. He treats it the way Eleanor taught him to: as something that compounds.

David Wilson (Rosie's son, born London ~1973) died of overdose in 2008. His Walker trust distribution — significantly larger and more front-loaded than Alex's, reflecting Eleanor's pre-David approach before she learned the lesson of what unstructured wealth does — transferred into AW Holdings.

David's distribution was the mistake Eleanor built Alex's graduated structure to correct. David received a large lump sum at 21 (~1994). Despite his addiction, David burned through his allowance, not his capital. The principal sat in professionally managed accounts, compounding from ~1994 to 2008. **Estimated value at transfer into AW Holdings: ~$60M.**

### 2021 — Alex Turns 18

**Walker trust distribution #1: $5M** — entered AW Holdings.

**NYC townhouse transfer:** The Walker Holdings-owned NYC townhouse, rarely used by Homer and Rosie, was transferred into AW Holdings (held through the newly created AW Estates subsidiary) as part of Alex's 18th birthday. Homer and Rosie never liked it much, rarely visited. Alex was starting Juilliard and needed a home. Practical, not extravagant. **Estimated asset value: ~$20-25M.**

**Alex's decision:** On receiving the parental inheritance — now substantially grown through 18 years of compounding — Alex understood exactly what unstructured wealth did to his father. He asked Eleanor to keep the entire fund under professional management within AW Holdings. He did not withdraw it. He did not celebrate it. He asked Eleanor to make sure it kept compounding. This is the moment Eleanor knew David's story would not repeat.

**Alex begins purchasing from the fund:** Art, vintage fashion, first editions, collectables. All acquisitions logged as AW Holdings assets. See AW_COLLECTION.md.

### 2024 — Alex Turns 21

**Walker trust distribution #2: $10M** — entered AW Holdings.

**Property transfers to AW Estates:** On his 21st birthday (June 28, 2024), Rosie and Homer gifted two additional properties to Alex, both transferred into AW Holdings through the AW Estates subsidiary:

- **Kensington townhouse, London** (Knightsbridge area, ~4,800 sq ft, six floors): Alex uses this when in London for work. Part of his life now. **Estimated value: ~£12-15M (~$15-19M USD).**

This was quieter than the bourbon barrel reveal, but more significant. Rosie and Homer simply decided Alex should own the homes he actually uses. The compound in Woodford County, Laurel Canyon, and Napa remain Walker Holdings assets.

**AW Estates established:** The subsidiary was formalized to hold all three real estate assets as a coherent portfolio, with dedicated property management infrastructure.

### Collection Register

All tangible and collectible assets are maintained in a formal Collection Register — a comprehensive inventory catalogued by Edward Cahill's team with annual independent valuation by Christie's Private Sales advisory service. Each item is logged with: acquisition date, purchase price, current insured value, provenance documentation, physical location, and condition notes.

The Register serves three functions:

- **Insurance:** Consolidated coverage across all properties. When a painting moves from the NYC townhouse to Kensington, the Register updates and insurance follows.
- **Estate planning:** Diane Prescott (General Counsel) uses the Register for tax and inheritance planning. The bourbon barrel collection alone requires specialist valuation.
- **Acquisition tracking:** When Alex texts Eleanor about a piece at Phillips, Edward's team checks the Register for portfolio concentration (is Alex overweight in photography? Does he need to diversify?) before executing. Eleanor rarely overrides Alex's taste — the Register is informational, not restrictive.

The Register does not include Alex's personal wardrobe (current-season clothing he actually wears), personal electronics, or everyday items. It covers: art, vintage/archive fashion pieces, watches above $5K replacement value, first editions and rare books, the bourbon barrel collection, and formally registered gifts from pre-18.

Alex has never seen the Register. He knows Eleanor's team tracks his purchases. He doesn't know there's a formal document with Christie's advisory involvement.
