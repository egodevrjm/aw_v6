## THE DAWSON CONNECTION

**The Family:** Richard Dawson, 7th Earl of Fletching, and Margaret, Countess of Fletching (née Harbury). Estate: Fletching Park, East Sussex. Old Anglican family (earldom created 1689). Modern wealth from investment banking overlaid on estate income.

**Sarah's Background:** Third of six children. Roedean (boarding school, age 8). Inward, quiet, drifting. Met David Wilson in London's late-90s party scene — he was handsome, wild, magnetic, Rosie Walker's son. Whirlwind marriage (possibly Vegas, without telling families). The Earl's family regarded it as vulgar, reckless. David's addiction confirmed their worst fears.

**Sarah's death (2003):** Childbirth complications. Died at 27, delivering Alex. The Dawsons didn't disown — they simply stopped calling. They closed the door quietly and permanently.

**Alex's early knowledge:** He knew about his mother's family growing up — met an aunt or two when young, knew the broad shape of it — but the Dawsons were distant figures, not presences.

**The Reconnection (Westminster, age 16-18):** Everything changed at Westminster School (London, ages 16-18).

- Met his cousins properly for the first time: **Jonathan Dawson** (born 2002, eldest son of the Viscount Hartfield — the Earl's heir, courtesy title "The Honourable Jonathan Dawson") and **Lucas Ashby** (born 2003, son of Lady Caroline Ashby née Dawson, Sarah's younger sister). Both at Westminster. Through the cousins, Alex met the Earl and Countess.

**The Reunion Dynamic:** The grandparents are not warm the way Rosie is — they will never be that. But they were impressed despite themselves. Alex at 16: 6'2" and still growing, athletic, four languages, reads classical literature and Italian poetry for pleasure, impeccable manners, carrying himself with an ease that comes from Rosie's world but maps perfectly onto theirs. He is, quietly and uncomfortably, more Dawson than David ever was — which is both the thing that draws them in and the thing that makes the loss of Sarah sharper.

**Current relationship:** Correct, measured, occasionally admiring. Christmas cards. The odd lunch when Alex is in London. Not cold, but held at a careful distance — by both sides.

**The cousins:** Warmer and looser than the grandparents, especially Lucas. Both now part of the London Lot. See LONDON_LOT.md for full detail.
