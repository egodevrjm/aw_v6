# HATFIELD BAR & GRILL

---

## THE OPENING PRINCIPLE

**Hatfield Bar & Grill is a national restaurant chain with international expansion underway — and Alex has been eating at the Lexington flagship since before he was old enough to order bourbon.**

This is not a celebrity restaurant. Not a vanity brand. Not a pop-up. It's a professionally operated, profitable, expanding hospitality company with 16 company-owned locations across the US and UK, an active franchise programme adding dozens of locations worldwide, and ambitions to go significantly further. The food is good. The bourbon programme is excellent. The brand is established and growing.

Alex relates to Hatfield Bar & Grill the way someone relates to a business their family owns. He's comfortable in every location, known at the flagship, and orders without looking at a menu. He doesn't run it, consult on it, or manage it. He eats there.

## THE BRAND

Hatfield Bar & Grill sits in the space between a neighbourhood bar and a fine dining restaurant. It's the kind of place where you can have a $15 burger and a Hatfield Original at the bar, or a $65 ribeye and a Cave Select pour in the dining room. The bourbon programme is the anchor — every location features the full Hatfield whiskey range plus a curated selection of other bourbons, American whiskeys, and a competent cocktail programme. The food is serious but not pretentious: Southern roots, seasonal menus, quality ingredients, kitchens that actually cook.

**Brand pillars:**
- Bourbon knowledge (staff trained, not theatrical)
- Quality food (kitchen-driven, not formula-driven)
- Warm hospitality (Southern roots, professional service)
- Comfortable atmosphere (established, not chasing trends)
- Consistent standard across locations (without feeling like a chain)

**What it's NOT:**
- Not a sports bar
- Not a brewpub
- Not a steakhouse chain pretending to be independent
- Not a "bourbon experience" tourist trap
- Not an Alex Wilson branded anything

## OPERATIONS

### Kitchen Standards

Every Hatfield Bar & Grill kitchen operates to the same standard:
- **Head Chef** at each location with menu authority within the Hatfield framework
- **Core menu** (~60% identical across locations): signature items that define the brand — the Hatfield burger, the short rib, the smoked chicken, the bourbon bread pudding
- **Regional menu** (~40% locally adapted): each Head Chef develops seasonal and regional items. New Orleans does crawfish. Nashville does hot chicken riff. San Francisco does local fish. London does Sunday roast.
- **Sourcing:** Local preferred, quality non-negotiable. Each location has relationships with regional suppliers.
- **Kitchen brigade:** Full professional kitchen in every location. Prep cooks, line cooks, sous chef, head chef. This is not heat-and-serve.

### Bourbon Programme

The anchor. What makes Hatfield Bar & Grill more than another American restaurant.

- **Full Hatfield range** at every location: Original, Small Batch, Single Barrel, Rye, Reserve (when allocated)
- **Curated bourbon selection:** 40-60 additional bourbons and American whiskeys. Staff-selected, rotated seasonally.
- **Cocktail programme:** Classic bourbon cocktails done correctly (Old Fashioned, Whiskey Sour, Mint Julep, Manhattan) plus seasonal originals. Not fussy. Not over-garnished. Competent.
- **Staff training:** Every bartender and server completes Hatfield bourbon education programme. Not theatrical — genuinely educated. They can talk mash bills, aging, proof, flavour profiles without performing it.
- **Neat pours standard:** When someone orders bourbon at Hatfield, the default assumption is they know what they're doing. No upselling, no condescension, no "are you sure you want that neat?"
- **Cave Select pours:** Available at flagship locations (Lexington, Nashville, NYC, London) when inventory allows. Priced by the ounce. Serious bourbon for serious drinkers.

### Service Standard

- Professional, warm, knowledgeable. Southern hospitality roots without Southern performance.
- Staff trained to treat regulars as regulars and new guests as welcome — not the other way around.
- No celebrity treatment protocols. If Alex walks in, staff greet him warmly because they know him (at Lexington) or because he has a reservation (at other locations). No fuss. No comps unless he's specifically hosted. He pays.
- Average meal time: 75-90 minutes. Hatfield doesn't rush tables.

## WHAT HATFIELD BAR & GRILL REPRESENTS

**For the Hatfield Group brand:** The public face. Most people who know Hatfield know it from the restaurants, not the distillery. The restaurants drive bourbon trial — someone tries Hatfield Single Barrel at dinner and buys a bottle at the liquor store. The chain is a marketing channel as much as a revenue stream.

**For Alex:** Comfort. Routine. Belonging. The Lexington flagship is his local — the place where nobody is performing anything, where the bartender knows his pour, where he can bring Tommy after polo or sit alone at the bar reading. Other locations are convenient extensions — the NYC spot when he's in Tribeca, the London spot when he's in Mayfair. But Lexington is home.

**For the expansion story:** Hatfield Bar & Grill is the growth engine. The franchise programme is already adding 15-20 locations per year. Combined with company-owned, Hatfield Bar & Grill will have 100+ locations worldwide within a few years. The brand is proven, the operational playbook is mature, and bourbon is globally ascendant. ## NARRATOR RULE — DO NOT EXPLAIN THE RESTAURANT IN SCENE

**Hatfield Bar & Grill is a place Alex goes. Not a brand to be introduced.**

When a scene is set at Hatfield, or when Alex mentions it, don't explain what it is. No ownership note. No brand description. No "the family's restaurant group." He's been going since he was a kid.

**Wrong:**
> They met at Hatfield Bar & Grill — one of sixteen locations in the family's hospitality group, the Lexington flagship that Alex had been coming to his whole life.

> They met at Hatfield. Alex had a corner table held.

In scene it's just where he goes in Lexington. Readers don't need the footnote.
