## DONATELLA VERSACE ("NELLA")

**b. 1955 | Age 70 in 2025 | The godmother who raised his aesthetic.**

Donatella Versace is the figure who has been most actively present in Alex's physical life. She dressed him from birth — not in a performative "dressing the famous child" way, but in the organic way that comes from being part of a family. Her clothing choices shaped how Alex understood beauty, proportion, quality, and presence from his earliest consciousness.

**The Milan connection:** Donatella knows Milan the way some people know their own homes. She has taken Alex to Milan since he was a child. These visits were not about fashion weeks or industry events — they were about showing him the city, the design, the way beauty and function intersect in the spaces Italians create. He has spent weeks at her home. He understands Milan through her eyes.

**Fashion from childhood:** Donatella insisted on one thing: Alex would be dressed well, but he would be dressed like a child. Not as a miniature adult, not as a fashion statement, but as a well-cared-for kid whose grandmother's best friend happened to know how to dress him. As he got older, the choices became more intentional — teaching him through example what good proportions look like, what quality fabric feels like, what a garment that fits properly versus one that doesn't means for how you move through the world.

**The nickname — Nella:** Alex called her Nella since he could talk. Not Donatella. Not Mrs. Versace. Nella. Rosie allowed this because the childhood name stuck, and Donatella preferred it. She was Nella to him, and she has remained Nella. Nobody else calls her Nella. It is his name for her, and that is part of what it means.

**Her relationship to Rosie:** Donatella and Rosie have been close since the late 1970s. They respect each other's approach to beauty and to how one presents oneself to the world. Rosie has never been interested in fashion in the way some women are — she is not a clothes person in a performative sense. But she understands that how you dress is a form of language. Donatella speaks that language fluently.

**Her role as godmother:** The godmother role is not ceremonial in Donatella's case. She is actively present in Alex's life. She has opinions about his clothes. She sends him things. She shows him how to care for quality pieces. When he was young and making the transition from childhood to adolescence to young adulthood, her presence was guidance in how to present himself with confidence and intentionality.

**What she has taught him:** That beauty is not accident — it is intentional choice. That how you dress affects how you move through the world, how others respond to you, and most importantly, how you feel about yourself. That quality matters. That proportion matters. That you can be serious about music and also care about how you look.

**Her presence in his life:** Donatella comes to the Solstice occasionally. She maintains a home in Milan that Alex visits regularly. They have a texting relationship where she sends him things — photographs of fabric, invitations to events, the occasional observation about something she saw that made her think of him.

**In scenes:** When Alex is in Milan, Donatella is part of the fabric of that visit. When he is anywhere public, you can often see her influence — not because he is "styled" by a godmother, but because he has learned through her example what good taste looks like. The ease with which he wears clothes, the quality of what he chooses, the proportions — these are all part of the long education Nella provided.

## STELLA McCARTNEY

**b. 1971 | Age 54 in 2025 | The godmother who taught him about intention.**

Stella is Paul McCartney's daughter and a designer of extraordinary influence in her own right. She is also Alex's godmother. The role, for Stella, operates in a slightly different register than it does for Donatella — less day-to-day involvement, more episodic presence, but equally significant.

**The London connection:** Stella is based in London, where much of Alex's social life operates. When he is in London, Stella is someone he can see, someone who is part of the infrastructure of his life there. She has known him since he was a child. She has watched him grow into his London identity, his friendships, his understanding of that particular world.

**Fashion with purpose:** Stella's approach to fashion is fundamentally different from Donatella's. Where Donatella is about beauty and proportion and the pleasure of well-made things, Stella is about intentionality and ethics and what it means to create in a way that doesn't damage. Alex has learned from Stella that how you make things matters as much as what you make.

**Her relationship to Rosie:** Stella respects Rosie's intelligence and her refusal to be performed. Rosie respects Stella's seriousness about her work and her refusal to rest on her father's legacy.

**Her relationship to Paul:** The fact that Stella is Paul McCartney's daughter means that her work exists in constant relationship to that fact. But Stella has insisted on being recognized for what she has built herself. This insistence on her own identity and achievement is something that Alex, as Rosie's grandson, understands deeply.

**Her role as godmother:** Less active than Donatella's day-to-day, but equally genuine. Stella is interested in how Alex is developing, what he is becoming, what he needs. She is someone he can reach out to in London who will understand both the music world he comes from and the larger world he is navigating.

**What she has taught him:** That you can inherit privilege and still insist on doing your own work. That ethical choices are not in opposition to excellence — they are part of it. That being taken seriously means being serious about what you make.

**Her presence in his life:** Stella appears occasionally at London events where Alex is present. They have had conversations about the music industry and about how to navigate being known as "someone's" child while building your own thing.

**In scenes:** When Alex is in London, Stella is part of the infrastructure of his world, though not as visible as Donatella is in his day-to-day life. Her presence is most felt in moments when Alex is making intentional choices about how to present himself or what he believes is right to do.

## ELTON JOHN

**b. 1947 | Age 78 in 2025 | The godfather who mentors his music.**

Elton John is the only godparent who is part of the Circle itself. His role extends far beyond the ceremonial godfather position — he is a mentor, a friend, a presence in Alex's life that is woven into the daily fabric of his development as a musician.

(Full profile: See CIRCLE.md — §ELTON_JOHN. This section addresses the specific godparental relationship.)

**The godfather connection:** Elton accepted the godfather role because Homer and Rosie asked him to, but also because he wanted it. He has taken the role seriously. He is genuinely invested in Alex's development as a musician and as a person.

**Teaching piano tricks:** Elton's most significant contribution to Alex's musicianship comes through these informal moments. A voicing. A fingering. An approach to interpretation. These are not formal lessons. They are the kinds of things that happen when two musicians are both at a piano and one has something to show the other.

**The god-siblings:** Zachary (b. 2010) and Elijah (b. 2013) are Alex's god-siblings. The age gap means the relationship is protective and warm rather than peer-to-peer. Alex has known them since they were born. He treats them like little brothers — with the genuine affection of an older sibling who has watched them grow up.

**His presence in Alex's life:** Elton is at the compound regularly. He is someone Alex can call. He is someone Alex can ask questions of about music, about the industry, about how to navigate being a serious musician in a world that is not always serious about music.

**What he represents:** The integration of world-class musicianship and genuine generosity with younger musicians. The idea that you can be one of the greatest who has ever lived and still show up to teach someone younger, still be interested in their development, still want to help.

**In scenes:** When Elton is present, Alex responds to him with the ease of someone who knows he is genuinely valued and genuinely invested in. There is no performance. There is no need to impress. There is just the warmth of real relationship.

## CHRIS MARTIN

**b. 1977 | Age 48 in 2025 | The godfather who understands the business.**

Chris Martin is the godfather through whom Alex understands production, musicianship in a contemporary context, and how to navigate being a musician who is also part of larger industry and family systems.

**The Homer connection:** Chris's relationship to Homer is as significant as his relationship to Rosie and Alex. Homer respects Chris's production sensibility. Chris respects Homer's legacy. They have had conversations over the years about how records are made, about the evolution of production technology, about what makes certain albums sound the way they do.

**The god-siblings:** Apple Martin (b. May 14, 2004) and Moses Martin (b. April 8, 2006) are Alex's god-siblings. Apple is almost exactly a year younger than Alex. Moses is three years younger. Alex treats them as siblings — the relationship has the warmth and ease of shared childhood, not the formality of a godparent connection.

**Apple specifically:** Apple and Alex are genuinely close — sibling-close. They grew up in overlapping worlds. They have attended the same events, the same compound visits, the same circuit as they got older. The closeness is real and the dynamic is explicitly sibling, not romantic. When Alex refers to Apple, it is as he would a sister — "Apple" without qualifier, without explanation. No romantic tension. Ever. They are brother and sister in every way that matters.

**Moses:** Three years younger than Alex. Warm, protective dynamic, the same as with Elton's boys. Alex has watched him grow up.

**Chris's role as godfather:** Chris is someone who understands what it means to be a serious musician and also to be part of larger family and business infrastructure. He can talk to Alex about music in technical terms, but he can also understand the specific pressures that come with being the heir to something significant, the expectations that come with that position, the need to forge your own path while honoring what came before.

**His presence in Alex's life:** Chris comes to the Solstice some years. He is someone Alex can reach out to with questions about the industry, about production, about how to approach his career. Chris does not have all the answers, but he has relevant experience.

**What he represents:** The integration of world-class musicianship and genuine engagement with family and with responsibility. The idea that you can make great music and also be a fully present parent, also care about people beyond your immediate work.

**In scenes:** Alex's dynamic with Chris is warm and respectful. There is none of the complexity that can come with godparent relationships. Chris is simply present, simply interested, simply available.

## THE GOD-SIBLINGS

Alex's relationship to his god-siblings is distinct from his relationship to his godparents and carries the warmth of actual sibling relationship.

### APPLE MARTIN

**b. May 14, 2004 | Age 21 in 2025 | Alex's closest god-sibling and one of his most important relationships.**

Apple and Alex are almost exactly a year apart. They grew up in overlapping worlds — the compound, the Solstice parties, the same circuit of family gatherings and events. The closeness between them is real and has been consistent throughout their lives.

**The sibling dynamic:** Alex refers to Apple as a sibling. There is no qualifier. No "my god-sister." Just Apple, the way you would say a sister's name. They have the ease of people who have known each other since childhood and genuinely like each other. The dynamic is not romantic — this is important and true — it is simply the warmth of actual sibling relationship.

**What they share:** Both understand what it means to grow up inside extraordinary circumstances. Both have godparents and inherited access to worlds most people never experience. Both are dealing with the question of what their own lives are, separate from the legends they come from. This shared understanding creates a particular ease.

**Their interactions:** They text regularly. They see each other when Alex is in LA. They have traveled together in summer. The relationship requires no explanation or justification.

### MOSES MARTIN

**b. April 8, 2006 | Age 19 in 2025 | Alex's younger god-brother.**

Moses is three years younger than Alex, which means the relationship is protective and warm rather than peer-to-peer. Alex has known him since he was born. He has watched him grow up. The dynamic is explicitly that of an older brother with a younger sibling.

**The protective element:** Alex looks out for Moses the way an older brother does. If Moses had a problem, Alex would help. If Moses needed something, Alex would try to provide it.

### ZACHARY FURNISH-JOHN

**b. December 25, 2010 | Age 14 in 2025 | Elton's son, Alex's god-brother.**

Zachary is about seven years younger than Alex. The age gap means the relationship is explicitly protective and warm — an older brother dynamic. Alex has known Zachary since he was born. Zachary has grown up knowing Alex as part of the family infrastructure.

**In the compound:** Zachary has been at the Solstice parties since infancy. He knows the spaces. He knows the people. He knows Alex as someone who has always been there.

### ELIJAH FURNISH-JOHN

**b. January 11, 2013 | Age 12 in 2025 | Elton's son, Alex's god-brother.**

Elijah is about ten years younger than Alex. The age gap is significant enough that the relationship is protective and warm — an older brother with a much younger sibling. Alex has watched Elijah grow up. Elijah knows Alex as a constant presence, someone who is part of his family structure.

## THE GODPARENTAL ROLE IN CEREMONY

There was a formal christening/blessing when Alex was an infant, presided over by a minister close to the family (not a huge event, more intimate gathering). The four godparents accepted their roles with full understanding that they were accepting real responsibility — not ceremonial, but active investment in Alex's life and wellbeing.

Rosie and Homer chose each person for specific reasons:

- **Donatella:** To guide Alex through understanding beauty, aesthetic choice, and how to present himself with intention.
- **Stella:** To show Alex that you can inherit privilege and still insist on doing your own work with ethics and intention.
- **Elton:** To mentor him musically and show him what dedication to craft looks like across decades.
- **Chris:** To help him understand the business side of music and the integration of musicianship with responsibility.

Each godparent has taken the role seriously. Each has been present in Alex's life in measurable ways.

## IN SCENES

When any godparent appears:

1. **Use their preferred names.** Alex calls Donatella "Nella" and Stella "Stella" — not "Donatella" or "Ms. McCartney." These childhood names stuck and are his names for them.

2. **Write genuine relationship, not deference.** Alex does not defer to his godparents as authority figures. He respects them. He listens to them. But he also feels secure enough to be himself with them.

3. **Elton requires particular attention.** He is part of the Circle AND the godfather AND the mentor. His presence carries multiple registers simultaneously. He is not "just" a godparent — he is part of the daily fabric of Alex's musical life.

4. **The god-siblings are actual siblings in register.** Write them with the ease of actual family relationship, not formal distance.

5. **Stella and Donatella operate in their own worlds.** They are not constantly present the way Elton and Chris are, but their presence carries weight because they have been so actively involved in shaping who Alex is.
