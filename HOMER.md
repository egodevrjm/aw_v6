## THE SCALE

**The greatest record producer and sound engineer who has ever lived.**

Think George Martin — if George Martin had also gone on to produce for some of the biggest acts in the world over the next 40 years, *and* married and produced the greatest musical artist alive.

Homer was in the room at Abbey Road in his early twenties, engineering Beatles albums, conducting orchestras on their records. Then he kept going — Bowie, Floyd, Queen, Elton, Fleetwood Mac, Emmylou, decades of Nashville's best.

**He did not peak at Abbey Road. Abbey Road is where he started.**

| Metric | Figure |
|--------|--------|
| **Albums produced/engineered** | 200+ |
| **Grammy Awards** | 15 (including 4× Best Engineered Album) |
| **Grammy Trustees Award** | 2010 |
| **Knighthood** | 1998 (KBE) |

**Industry terminology:** "Wilson mid-range," "Wilson room," "Wilson sound" — taught in music schools worldwide. He is the reason several of the most important albums ever made sound the way they do.

## EARLY LIFE & TRAINING

**Royal Academy of Music:** Violin and conducting degrees. Trained conductor (baton technique). Could have conducted orchestras — chose the booth instead.

**Why the booth:** He heard things other people didn't. Perfect pitch (within 3 cents, same as Rosie, same as Alex). The ear drove everything.

**Abbey Road:** Junior engineer by 21. George Martin needed a trained conductor for complex orchestrations on Beatles albums ("A Day in the Life," "Eleanor Rigby") — Homer, 22, with Royal Academy baton technique, got the call.

**Career trajectory:** Abbey Road → Los Angeles session work → Nashville → Kentucky (permanently, 1980 onwards, by choice).

## PHONEIX HOLLOW & THE COMPOUND

Homer did NOT design the compound's structure.

**What Homer designed:** Everything sonic.

- The studio's acoustics and specifications (built 1982)
- Listening rooms in London and NYC townhouses (soundproofing, acoustic treatment)
- All acoustic design in the main house (where sound behaves properly)

**What Homer did NOT design:** Pool, property layout, guest cottages, main house structure, landscaping, plumbing, sightlines. That's architects and contractors across 45 years.

**Critical narrator rule:** If something involves how sound behaves in a space, Homer may have had input. If it involves building, structure, landscape, or design — that's not Homer's domain.

**The studio:** Never gone quiet since 1982. Generations of artists have worked there. The "Homer room" specification has become industry standard. Young engineers make pilgrimages specifically to work in Homer-designed spaces.

## TRAVEL & CURRENT LOCATION

**Travel pattern change (present day):** Homer and Rosie don't travel internationally anymore. Both are 80/81 — healthy and sharp, but transatlantic trips have effectively stopped.

**Last international trip:** 2023-2024 (two weeks visiting Alex in London during his RAM junior year exchange). Stayed at the Kensington townhouse while Alex was there. That was the exception. It won't become a pattern again.

**US base — three-way rotation:**
- **Phoenix Hollow (Woodford County, Kentucky):** Primary home. The default. Where they are most of the year. The compound is theirs — staff, studio, horses, the rhythm of a place they've lived in for 45 years.
- **Napa (Hatfield Wines estate):** The private residence on the Hatfield Wines estate, east of St. Helena. A Hatfield Group asset, not Walker Holdings — but Rosie and Homer use it as a personal retreat. More land, more privacy, quieter than Laurel Canyon. Homer likes the scale. When they're there, it's genuinely low-key: no staff events, no visitors, just the vineyards.
- **Laurel Canyon (Los Angeles):** The historical property. Rosie and Homer still use the home studio. They stay when LA has something — industry events, old friends, Circle business that requires a West Coast base.

**NYC:** They don't maintain their own NYC base anymore. When something brings them to New York — the Met Gala is the clearest example — they stay with Alex at the townhouse. His 4th floor guest suite is effectively theirs for these visits. They don't stay long. Homer has never found a reason to enjoy New York for more than a week at a time.

**The international properties (London, Sardinia) transferred to Alex at 21 precisely because Homer and Rosie no longer use them.** There was no point in Walker Holdings maintaining Kensington and Villa Serena for two people who won't cross the Atlantic. Alex will. The transfer was the obvious move.

**The properties (Alex's, as of his 21st birthday):**
- **Kensington townhouse:** Functionally Alex's in terms of daily use. Homer has about three paintings in it total. Alex has put his mark on it.
- **Villa Serena (Sardinia):** The family's Mediterranean property since 1986. Homer built it. Now Alex's.

## ROSIE & HOMER

Met Abbey Road, 1970. He was 26, she 25.

**The moment:** He stopped a take and told her the mic placement was wrong. Nobody had told Rosie Walker she was doing something wrong. She said "Show me." They married in 1971.

**The partnership:** Moved to California together mid-70s. Retired together in 1980 by mutual choice.

> "The greatest artist married the greatest engineer. Two kinds of genius under one roof."

They have been together 55 years. They still are.
