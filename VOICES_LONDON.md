# VOICES_LONDON — LONDON LOT & WESTMINSTER CIRCLE

**Complete voice profiles for the London Lot (the ~50-60 member London social circle that formed around Alex during the Westminster years and RAM year) and the Westminster Boys social group. These are Alex's London friends — people he sees regularly when in Kensington, meets for dinner, crosses paths at events, and stays connected to through London Lot WhatsApp and Westminster Boys chats.**

| Sample | Context |
|--------|---------|

## VOICE CONSTRUCTION FOR LONDON LOT SCENES

### Group Chat (LL WhatsApp ~50-60 people)

**What happens:** Plans form and reform. Someone suggests a dinner, another suggests a different restaurant, Cosima judges both. Hugo offers to book somewhere. A discussion of weekend plans. Frequent references to Glastonbury (in season), exhibitions, clubs (Annabel's, Chiltern Firehouse, Soho House).

**Cast rotation critical:** Do not write the same 4-5 voices every time. 50-60 people are in the chat. Use them. See GROUP_CHATS_DETAIL.md for the CAST ROTATION PROTOCOL.

**Alex's participation:** Medium-high. Posts plans, responds to direct tags, drops one-liners occasionally. Not driving chaos.

**Tone:** London-sophisticated without trying. References to galleries, restaurants, polo, music. Slightly self-aware humour. Nobody performing for an audience.

### Westminster Boys Chat (WB WhatsApp ~10-12)

**What happens:** Faster, more banter. More inside jokes. School references. Plans that involve polo or country weekends or both.

**Alex's participation:** Medium-high. More active than in LL. Warmer.

**Tone:** School-friend affection. Boys who've known each other for years. Less performed than LL.

### In-Person (Kensington, Mayfair, Clubs)

**Register shift:** More verbal. Conversations that go deeper. The intimacy of people who've chosen to spend time together.

**When Alex arrives:** No special register shift. Warmth increases, but he's treated like a friend, not a celebrity. The beauty is noted as fact (especially by newer acquaintances) then moves forward as topic.

## CRITICAL NOTES

**Do not write London Lot members as star-struck.** They've been around Alex for years. The wonder ended at hello.

**Do not write them as performing for press.** These chats and dinners are private.

**Do not write them as simpering or deferential.** They are confident people from secure families. They treat Alex as an equal.

**Do not overuse the specific voice cards.** Cosima shouldn't deliver aesthetic judgements every message. Freddie shouldn't start every chat planning a pub. Build from context.

**Do not homogenize.** A 50-60 person chat should feel like a room. Different people talking, different tones, different subjects rotating.
