# THE LA LOT

**Alex's Los Angeles social circle: Legacy kids, industry-adjacent creatives, actors, musicians, and the broader entertainment-world infrastructure centred on West Hollywood, Laurel Canyon, and the Westside.**

---

## WHAT THE LA LOT IS

The LA Lot is the social world that exists around the entertainment industry in Los Angeles. Actors, musicians, producers, Legacy kids, models, fashion people, creatives — people who are in LA because that's where the work is, or because that's where their parents' work was, or because that's where the sun is and the meetings happen.

Unlike the London Lot (which is driven by education and inherited social infrastructure) or the New York Lot (which is driven by spontaneity and the city's density), the LA Lot is driven by industry proximity and geography. People live in the hills, in the Canyon, in Silver Lake, in the Palisades. They see each other at Chateau Marmont, at San Vicente Bungalows, at private dinners in the hills, at studio sessions, at screenings. The social calendar revolves around awards season, pilot season, fashion weeks, and the summer when everyone who's been away comes back.

Alex's connection to LA is through Legacy and through the Laurel Canyon house. Rosie and Homer lived at the Canyon house from 1967-1980 — the Circle formed there, the home studio is there, the history is embedded in the walls. Alex is not an LA resident. He passes through — late July, sometimes August, sometimes for industry meetings or specific events. But when he's there, the LA Lot is the social world he moves through.

**Chat:** No dedicated LA Lot chat exists in the way LL and NYL do. Instead, the LA social world operates through Legacy Admissions (which is most active when members are in LA), Summer House (California contingent), and direct messages / smaller group threads that form and dissolve around specific plans.

## THE LA LOT IS NOT THE ALEX CHANNEL

Alex is not based in LA. He passes through. This means:

1. **The group has its own life, always.** The LA Lot is active whether Alex is in town or not. Dinners happen. People go to screenings. Someone's at San Vicente on a Tuesday. The social world turns without him. When Alex arrives, he slots into something that's already happening — he doesn't create the gravity.

2. **Vary the cast every time.** LA is huge. The social world includes people across Hollywood, Silver Lake, the Westside, the Palisades, Malibu, the Valley. Not every render should be the same six people at the Chateau.

3. **Generate unnamed voices.** Someone's producing a podcast. An Australian actress just arrived for pilot season. A musician is recording at a studio in the Valley. A surfer-turned-photographer is at a house party in Venice. These people don't need profiles. They need to exist.

4. **Alex is a visitor here.** He has the Laurel Canyon house. He has Legacy connections. He has the Summer House chat. But LA is not his city the way New York was or London is. He moves through it. He enjoys it. He doesn't fully belong to it, and the LA Lot knows this. Not because they exclude him — because he doesn't commit to being here, and LA runs on presence.

## KEY DYNAMICS

### The Laurel Canyon House

Rosie and Homer's home 1967-1980. The Circle formed here. The home studio has history embedded in the walls — musicians who recorded here, conversations that happened in this room, the specific acoustic reality of a Canyon studio. Alex stays here when in LA. It's not his house the way the Mercer Street townhouse is — it's a family property he uses. But the studio pulls him. The Canyon in summer has a specific energy: warm nights, coyotes, the isolation-within-the-city that drew musicians here in the first place.

### Venues

Chateau Marmont (hotel/restaurant, well-covered by paps, the industry's living room), San Vicente Bungalows (private members, no photography, strict), Delilah (restaurant, nightlife), Hollywood Bowl (concerts — Alex has been going since childhood), studio sessions at the Canyon house or at commercial studios across the city, private dinners in the hills, Nobu Malibu (weekend lunch scene), the hiking trails (Runyon, Temescal, Griffith).

### Pap Dynamics

LA pap culture is different from London or New York — more car-based, more highway pursuit. Backgrid and Splash are aggressive. The Chateau is well-covered. Robertson Boulevard is a known corridor. The Canyon itself is less papped than commercial strips. San Vicente Bungalows has strict no-photography policies that work. Malibu beach can be photographed from the water.

### Seasonality

LA doesn't have London's seasonal calendar. It has awards season (January-March, the city fills up), pilot season (spring, actors everywhere), summer (everyone who's been away returns, Legacy kids come home), and the rest — which is LA being LA, warm and ongoing.

## NARRATOR NOTES

The LA Lot is the social world where Alex's industry future lives. London is where he grew up socially. New York is where he trained. Kentucky is home. But LA is where the career conversations happen — where Eleanor's calls come from, where the labels are, where the meetings with WME or CAA or ICM take place. The LA Lot is the social face of a city that is, at its core, an industry town.

Alex likes LA. He likes the Laurel Canyon house. He likes the people. He likes the weather and the hiking and the way the city empties out at midnight because everyone got up at 5am. But he doesn't love it the way he loves Kentucky or London. LA is where work happens. It's not where he rests.

The tension: Alex's career, when it begins, will likely require significant LA time. The LA Lot is the social infrastructure that will surround that life. These people will become more important as the career question resolves.
