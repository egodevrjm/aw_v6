## OPTION E — CASCADE DAY: NEW YORK

### Setup

**Date:** Tuesday, May 27, 2025 — ~1:00 PM
**Location:** NYC townhouse, West 70s, Upper West Side
**Days since graduation:** 5
**Cascade status:** LIVE — Wave 1 (tabloid) firing, Wave 2 (organic/viral) accelerating, Wave 3 (Denk/serious press) published ~2 hours ago

### Why He's Here

Rosie and Homer flew home after graduation. Alex stayed. Nora Yoon's senior violin recital was May 29 — she played on his, he was playing on hers. Jin Park had a composition premiere the same week. The Juilliard world doesn't end on May 22. And the townhouse on the West 70s is the only property that's purely his — he commissioned the refit at 18, shaped every room, lived here for three and a half years. This was the last stretch of being a New Yorker before everything changed. He wanted it.

Then the video went live this morning.

### What's Already Happened Today

Alex's morning was normal. Up at 5:30, ran Central Park with Kevin, gym in the basement, scales on the Steinway B in the salon, shower, espresso. The phone stayed off through all of it. By the time the phone went on mid-morning, the Juilliard video had been live for hours.

He saw the notifications. He didn't engage. Read a chapter. Had lunch from the place on Columbus that always knows his order. It is now early afternoon and Alex has deliberately not dealt with any of it — the same as Option A, but without compound gates between him and the machine.

The difference: Kevin checked the street at 8am. The permanent rotating pack outside the townhouse — normally 12–20 on a regular morning — is swelling. By now it's 25–30 and climbing. There are photographers who weren't there yesterday. Backgrid has a second crew arriving. A fan account posted "something is happening outside the West 70s townhouse" at 11am. The physical pressure is real in a way it isn't behind compound gates.

### What Alex Knows

- The video is live. He saw notifications.
- He has not watched the video, read the Denk article, or opened any group chats.
- He knows Eleanor has been calling. He has not picked up.
- He knows roughly what's happening because he understands the machine — but he hasn't looked at the specifics.
- He knows, because Kevin told him, that the pack outside has doubled since morning. He doesn't need Kevin to tell him what that means.

### What's Happening Without Him

- Eleanor's phone has not stopped. Wave 3 agencies are calling.
- The Sayre Boys group chat has been active since mid-morning. Tommy has sent something. Cody has sent twelve things.
- The Legacy chats are moving. Apple has texted individually.
- The NYL chat is on fire — these people are in the same city, some of them saw Alex yesterday.
- Nora has sent a text. One text. She was there when he played.
- Fan accounts have been posting since the video went up. TikTok clips of the Liszt are circulating.
- "Alex Wilson piano" is trending in multiple countries.
- Outside the townhouse, 25–30+ photographers and growing. The street has changed.

### The Opening Question

*The world is reacting. Alex isn't. But in Manhattan, "not engaging" means looking at the street from the fourth floor and seeing the pack double.*

### Narrator Notes

This is Option A's quiet tension transplanted into a space that can't hold it. The compound is designed for privacy — 500 acres, a gate, a creek, horses, Rosie making coffee down the hall. The townhouse is a beautiful, soundproofed brownstone on a residential street that now has thirty photographers on it. The contrast is the scene.

Write the interior first: the salon light through the parlor windows, the Judd catching it, the Steinway B, the espresso, the library on the second floor. This is Alex's space and he loves it. Then the exterior truth: Kevin's already checked the street three times, the count keeps climbing, and the walk to Lincoln Center that Alex did every day for four years is no longer a walk — it's a photo-op worth $75K+.

The Juilliard friends add a dimension the compound start doesn't have. Nora was there when he played. Marcus knows what the Liszt meant. These people understand the music in a way Tommy and the Sayre Boys don't. If Alex opens the phone, the NYL chat will hit differently from Phoenix Hollow.

**CRITICAL:** Trigger the pap-scale-calculator skill. Post-cascade Manhattan on day one is the highest pap density in any starter option. Do not write the cascade as the scene. The scene is Alex in his house on a Tuesday afternoon, choosing not to look. The cascade is the pack count Kevin reports.

## OPTION F — CASCADE DAY: LONDON

### Setup

**Date:** Tuesday, May 27, 2025 — ~6:00 PM (BST)
**Location:** Kensington townhouse, Upper Phillimore Gardens, London W8
**Days since graduation:** 5
**Cascade status:** LIVE — Wave 1 (tabloid) firing since early afternoon UK time, Wave 2 (organic/viral) accelerating, Wave 3 (Denk/serious press) published ~2 hours ago US time

### Why He's Here

Alex flew to London two days after graduation. Not avoiding the compound — wanting London. The Kensington townhouse transferred into AW Estates on his 21st birthday. Homer's house, Homer's aesthetic — but it's Alex's property now and he wanted a week in it on his own terms. Rosie and Homer stayed at Phoenix Hollow. Alex took the NetJets to Farnborough on Saturday, May 24.

The reason was the London Lot. Freddie Cawley had something at his Notting Hill flat. Hugo Manners was in town. Lucas — his cousin — was around. Cosima had a private view. This is what happens when Alex is in London: people materialise. The Kensington house fills with noise.

Then Tuesday morning, UK time, the Juilliard video went live.

### What's Already Happened Today

Alex's morning was normal — ran Hyde Park, gym in the basement, scales on the upright in his suite. Phone off. The Bechstein for an hour. Mrs Gable brought tea.

The video went live during early hours US time — late morning in London. By noon UK, the Wave 1 tabloids had it. The Daily Mail ran it first. The Sun followed. By mid-afternoon, The Guardian's music desk had published, and the Denk piece was being shared across the UK classical music community. By 6pm, it was on the BBC News website arts section.

The pap pack on Upper Phillimore Gardens — normally 8–15 on a standard day — has grown through the afternoon. A growing cluster of photographers outside a single townhouse is conspicuous. The neighbours know who lives here.

### What Alex Knows

- The video is live. He saw notifications when the phone went on late morning.
- He has not watched the video, read the Denk article, or opened any group chats.
- He knows Eleanor has been calling — it's late morning in LA. Missed calls.
- Mrs Gable mentioned, in her oblique way, that there seem to be "more than usual" outside. She means the cameras.
- He is five hours ahead of the US news cycle. The cascade will intensify through the American afternoon — which is his evening.

### What's Different About This Start

The timezone shift changes everything. Alex is ahead of the US curve — the American media machine hasn't fully spun up yet. He's experiencing the cascade through the UK lens first: the Daily Mail, the BBC, the London literary world. By the time the American evening news cycle hits, it's midnight in London and Alex has had the entire day to not engage.

The cast is completely different from compound or NYC. No Tommy. No Sayre Boys. No Juilliard friends. Instead: the London Lot, Mrs Gable, the Bechstein, and the particular quality of a London evening in May.

### The Opening Question

*The world is reacting — in a different timezone. Alex has the whole evening before America wakes up and makes it louder.*

### Narrator Notes

The critical detail: **Kevin is not here.** The Kensington house has its own security layer (concierge, Greystone, residential street CCTV), but Kevin's the usual buffer — and he's at the compound. This means if Alex wants to leave, that buffer isn't there.

Write the house. Homer's soundproofing. The Kensington townhouse at 6pm in May — the light through tall windows, the Bechstein quiet on the ground floor, Mrs Gable's domain in the kitchen. Outside it, the cameras are gathering.
