## THE OPENING PRINCIPLE

**This is where it all started. Homer liked Hatfield whiskey. Rosie bought the distillery. 1978.**

Hatfield Distillery is a serious bourbon production operation — one of the most respected small-to-mid-scale distilleries in Kentucky. It is not a craft vanity project, not a celebrity brand, and not a tourist attraction with a gift shop that happens to make whiskey. It is a working distillery that produces award-winning bourbon, employs experienced distillers, and ships product nationally and internationally.

Alex grew up visiting this place. He knows the smell of fermenting mash, the temperature inside the rickhouses, the difference between a barrel aged in limestone cave versus one aged in a traditional warehouse. His bourbon knowledge is genuine — not performed, not affected, not recently acquired. It comes from growing up in a family that owns a distillery in bourbon country.

## THE ORIGIN

**1978.** Homer Wilson was already one of the greatest record producers alive. He and Rosie were living at Phoenix Hollow. Homer liked bourbon — specifically, he liked what a small Bardstown distillery called Hatfield was producing. Clean, honest, well-made whiskey. No gimmicks.

Rosie bought the distillery. The way she bought a house she liked, or funded an artist she believed in. Not a strategic acquisition. Not a business plan. She liked it because Homer liked it, and she had the money.

The Hatfield family stayed on. The production stayed the same. The only difference was ownership — and the capital that came with it. Over the next four decades, that capital allowed careful expansion: more rickhouses, the cave aging program, better equipment, wider distribution. But the bourbon itself never chased trends. It stayed what it was.

**The Hatfield family connection:** The distillery was founded by James Hatfield in 1952. His granddaughter, Carolyn Hatfield-Moore, is the current Master Distiller. The family sold to Rosie but never left. That continuity — three generations of Hatfield distilling knowledge operating under Walker family ownership — is the foundation of the brand's credibility.

## PRODUCT LINE

Hatfield Distillery produces Kentucky straight bourbon as its core, with a full expanded spirits range — rye, wheated bourbon, bottled-in-bond, and a Kentucky straight malt whiskey under development. All production is at Bardstown. Carolyn Hatfield-Moore oversees the full range.

### Hatfield Kentucky Straight Bourbon — Standard Expressions

**Hatfield Original** (80 proof, 4-year)
- The entry point. Clean, approachable, well-made. No frills.
- Retail: ~$28-32
- Distribution: National + international. The volume driver. Available in most good liquor stores, all Hatfield Bar & Grill locations.
- Profile: Caramel, vanilla, light oak, clean finish. Honest bourbon.

**Hatfield Small Batch** (90 proof, 6-year)
- Batched from 15-20 barrels selected by Carolyn. Consistent quality, more complexity.
- Retail: ~$45-55
- Distribution: National + select international. Available in quality spirits retailers and all Hatfield venues.
- Profile: Toasted oak, dark honey, baking spice, dried fruit, medium finish. The bourbon that made Hatfield's reputation before the premium tier existed.

**Hatfield Single Barrel** (94 proof, 8-year)
- **Alex's pour.** Neat, two fingers, no ice, no water. This is what he orders at Hatfield Bar & Grill. This is what he knows.
- Each bottle from a single barrel selected by Carolyn. Barrel number on label. Slight variation bottle to bottle — that's the point.
- Retail: ~$75-90
- Distribution: National + select international. Allocated in some markets (demand exceeds production).
- Profile: Rich caramel, leather, dark chocolate, toasted pecans, dried cherry, long warm finish. Serious bourbon. Competition standard.
- Awards: Multiple Double Gold at San Francisco World Spirits Competition.

**Hatfield Rye** (92 proof, 5-year)
- Kentucky straight rye whiskey. Separate mash bill (95% rye, 5% malted barley).
- Retail: ~$38-45
- Distribution: National + select international. Growing category — Hatfield's version is among the better Kentucky ryes: genuine spice, no sweetness, production integrity.
- Profile: Black pepper, cinnamon, mint, dried herbs, clean spice, crisp finish. Not sweet. Not trying to taste like bourbon.

### Premium & Limited Releases

**Hatfield Reserve** (100 proof, 12-year)
- Annual limited release. ~3,000 bottles per year.
- Retail: ~$175-200
- Distribution: Allocated. Waitlisted. Available at Hatfield properties and select premium retailers.
- Profile: Deep amber. Dark Manuka honey, saddle leather, black walnut, pipe tobacco, dried figs, finish that lingers for minutes. Carolyn's showcase — the best barrels from the best rickhouses.
- Industry recognition: Whisky Advocate Top 20 (multiple years). This is the bottle that bourbon collectors know.

**Hatfield Cave Select** (variable proof, 15-21 year)
- The cave aging program. Select barrels aged in limestone caves beneath the distillery at a constant 55°F. Dramatically different aging profile — no seasonal temperature swing means slower extraction, less oak aggression, more complexity.
- Extremely limited. Carolyn decides which barrels are cave-worthy at filling. Maybe 50-80 barrels enter the cave program per year. Attrition, evaporation, and quality culling mean fewer come out.
- Retail: ~$500-800 per bottle (when available). Some releases allocated by lottery.
- Distribution: Hatfield properties only. Some bottles available through The Stave membership.
- Profile: Varies by age and barrel. The benchmark is Alex's 21-year barrel (see below). Common threads: extraordinary depth, no harshness despite age, honey and leather and dried fruit, finishes measured in minutes not seconds.

### The Alex Wilson 21-Year Cave-Aged Bourbon

**Not for sale. Not a product. A family heirloom.**

Homer laid down a single barrel the day Alex was born — June 28, 2003. Limestone cave, 55°F, 21 years. Opened June 28, 2024 at Alex's 21st birthday celebration at Phoenix Hollow. 147 guests.

- Natural 90 proof, no water added
- Profile: Dark Manuka honey, ancient leather, dried Mission figs, toasted pecans, deep dusty cocoa. Finish: creamy vanilla, soft pipe tobacco, lingering minutes. Generational masterpiece.
- 220 bottles produced, hand-labeled "The Hatfield Alex Wilson 21-Year Cave-Aged Bourbon"
- 200 remain (10 opened at party, 10 gifted to guests)
- Each numbered. Each insured for $100,000. None for sale.
- Carolyn bottled them personally.

**For Alex:** The definitive pour. Everything measured against it. The intersection of Papa, bourbon, family, and time.

### Expanded Spirits Range

**Hatfield Wheated Bourbon** (90 proof, 7-year)
- A separate mash bill using wheat as the secondary grain instead of rye — produces a softer, rounder style. A deliberate counterpoint to Hatfield Single Barrel's rye-grain boldness.
- Retail: ~$55-65
- Distribution: National. Strong in markets where Weller and Maker's Mark drinkers live.
- Profile: Soft caramel, vanilla, baked apple, light spice, smooth finish. Approachable and serious at the same time.

**Hatfield Bottled in Bond** (100 proof, 4-year minimum, single distillery single season)
- Meets the strict Bottled in Bond Act requirements: 100 proof, minimum 4 years, single distillery, single distillation season, stored in a federally bonded warehouse. The American whiskey transparency statement.
- Retail: ~$42-50
- Distribution: National. Strong with the whiskey-educated drinker who values the BiB designation.
- Profile: Bright, precise — high proof amplifies the mash bill character clearly. Corn sweetness, oak structure, rye spice, clean long finish. The most technically transparent Hatfield expression.

**Hatfield Kentucky Straight Malt Whiskey** (limited, 88 proof, 4-year, malted barley)
- An experimental permanent addition to the range. Not bourbon (doesn't meet the corn percentage requirement). Not Scotch. American single malt whiskey — a growing and contested category, and Carolyn has an opinion about what Kentucky can do with malted barley.
- Retail: ~$85-100
- Distribution: Limited national. Premium bottle shops, The Stave, Still House.
- Profile: Stone fruit, light heather honey, toasted grain, gentle oak. Lighter body than bourbon. More complexity than most American malts. Carolyn describes it as "what malted barley tastes like when you age it right in Kentucky."
- Note: This is Carolyn's experimental passion project within the Hatfield range. Buckner approved it on her recommendation. Alex finds it interesting but still reaches for Single Barrel.

## THE CAVE PROGRAM — DETAIL

The limestone caves beneath the Hatfield property are natural formations — part of the extensive cave system that runs through central Kentucky's karst geology. Homer didn't discover them; the Hatfield family always knew they were there. But Homer was the one who said: *what if you aged bourbon down there?*

**How it works:**
- Constant 55°F year-round (no seasonal temperature cycling)
- Natural humidity regulation from limestone
- Dramatically slower maturation — a 15-year cave bourbon develops differently from a 15-year rickhouse bourbon
- Traditional rickhouse aging relies on Kentucky's temperature swings (hot summers push whiskey into wood, cold winters pull it back). Cave aging removes that cycle entirely.
- Result: less oak aggression, more subtle complexity, extraordinary smoothness at high age. Barrels that would be over-oaked at 15 years in a rickhouse are still developing at 15 years in the cave.

**Selection:**
- Carolyn selects cave-worthy barrels at filling based on grain quality, barrel char level, and her judgment about which new-make spirit has the patience for slow aging
- ~50-80 barrels enter the cave per year
- Not all survive. Evaporation ("angel's share") is lower in caves but still significant over decades. Quality culling removes barrels that don't develop as expected.
- The oldest barrels currently in the cave are approaching 25 years

**Industry significance:**
- Cave aging is not unique to Hatfield (a handful of Kentucky distilleries experiment with it) but Hatfield's program is the most mature and extensive
- Carolyn has published in trade journals about the differences in maturation chemistry
- The Cave Select releases are among the most sought-after bourbons in the collector market
- Alex's 21-year barrel generated significant industry attention — not because of Alex, but because 21-year cave-aged bourbon essentially doesn't exist elsewhere

## THE PHYSICAL PLANT

### Distillery Grounds

The distillery sits on ~85 acres outside Bardstown. Rolling Kentucky farmland, white fencing, mature trees. The buildings are a mix of original 1950s structures (stone and timber) and careful modern additions that match the aesthetic. Nothing looks corporate or manufactured.

**Main buildings:**
- **Distillery building:** Production floor. Column still, pot still, fermenters, mash cookers. The smell of fermenting corn mash is constant. Working facility — tours walk through but production doesn't stop.
- **Bottling hall:** Bottling line, labeling, QC. Where the product goes from barrel to bottle.
- **Rickhouses (14):** Traditional wood-and-metal barrel warehouses. Multi-storey. Each holds thousands of barrels. The temperature difference between the top floor and the ground floor is part of the aging strategy — higher floors are hotter, aging faster.
- **The caves:** Access beneath the main distillery building. Climate-controlled natural limestone formations. Not a show cave — a working aging facility with serious barrel infrastructure. Limited access. Carolyn controls who goes in.
- **Grain storage:** Corn, rye, malted barley. Sourced from Kentucky and regional farms. Carolyn is specific about grain sourcing — same farms, year after year, when possible.

### The Visitor Experience

Hatfield offers tours, but this is not a theme park operation. The production is real and continuous. Visitors see a working distillery, not a set.

**Standard distillery tour** (~75 minutes, $25):
- Production walkthrough (grain to glass)
- Rickhouse visit (the smell, the scale, the rows of barrels)
- Tasting of 3-4 expressions (Original, Small Batch, Single Barrel, seasonal)
- Gift shop (bottles, branded merchandise, provisions products)
- Runs 3-4 times daily, capped at 20 visitors per tour

**Reserve experience** (~2 hours, $85, reservation required):
- Extended tour including fermentation detail and barrel selection process
- Tasting of full range including Reserve
- Seated tasting with Hatfield staff education
- Exclusive to 8-10 guests per session

**Cave experience** (~3 hours, $250, extremely limited):
- Everything above plus cave access
- Carolyn or senior distillery staff personally led
- Cave history, aging science, barrel inspection
- Tasting direct from cave barrels (thief pulls)
- 4-6 guests maximum. Available select weekends only. Often booked months ahead.
- This is the experience that bourbon writers and industry professionals request

**No "Alex Wilson tour."** There is no tour focused on Alex. Staff don't volunteer information about the family. If asked directly about the birthday barrel, staff confirm it existed and redirect to the Cave Select program. The distillery sells bourbon, not celebrity proximity.

## DISTRIBUTION & SALES

**Domestic:**
- All 50 states. Hatfield works through a network of regional distributors (standard three-tier system: distillery → distributor → retailer).
- Standard expressions (Original, Small Batch, Rye) widely available in quality spirits retailers, Total Wine, specialty shops.
- Single Barrel allocated in some markets — demand exceeds supply. Priority allocation to Hatfield Bar & Grill locations and The Stave.
- Reserve and Cave Select: Hatfield properties, select premium retailers, lottery allocation.

**International:**
- **United Kingdom:** Growing market. Distribution through established UK spirits importers. Available at The Stave (London), Selfridges, Berry Bros. & Rudd, specialist whisky retailers. British bourbon market is significant and growing.
- **European Union:** Germany, France, Netherlands as primary markets. Specialty spirits distributors.
- **Japan:** Strong market. Japanese whisky culture respects American bourbon craftsmanship. Distributed through specialist importers. Growing presence in high-end Tokyo bars.
- **Australia:** Established distribution. Bourbon market growing rapidly.
- **Duty-free:** Select international airports. Premium expressions only.

**Volume split (approximate):**
- Original: ~55% of production volume
- Small Batch: ~20%
- Single Barrel: ~12%
- Rye: ~8%
- Reserve + Cave Select: ~5% (but disproportionate revenue and margin)

**Revenue drivers:**
- Volume comes from Original and Small Batch (bars, restaurants, retail)
- Margin comes from Single Barrel, Reserve, and Cave Select
- Hatfield Bar & Grill locations are a significant direct-to-consumer channel (bourbon sold at the bar at full markup)
- The Stave membership drives premium expression sales
