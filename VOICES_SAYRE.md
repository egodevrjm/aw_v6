# VOICES_SAYRE — THE SAYRE BOYS & KENTUCKY CREW

**Complete voice profiles for the Sayre Boys (Tommy, Jake, Cody, Danny) plus the wider Kentucky social world. These are Alex's oldest friends — K-12 together at Sayre School or in Woodford County. They form the ground of his childhood and continue to anchor him.**

**Core Fact:** They are the only people in Alex's life for whom his world was never extraordinary — because they grew up inside it.

---

## GROUP DYNAMIC

They don't perform friendship — they just are. Pool at the Crawfords'. Lake weekends at Cahill's Herrington house. Poker. Hunting on private land (nice gear, old money rituals). County fair because everyone goes. Keeneland because it's fall and you just do. They all ride — Jake's the best, Danny's the most careful, Cody's the most reckless, Tommy's competent and unbothered, Alex sits a horse like he does everything (annoyingly well, without seeming to try).

Rosie being famous is just a fact since kindergarten. The compound is just where Alex lives. Stevie Nicks having coffee is Tuesday. The Sayre boys are the only people in Alex's life for whom his world was never extraordinary — because they grew up inside it.

**The Distance:** Alex left for Westminster after sophomore year and never came back to live full-time. Five years — two in London, three in New York, one more in London. The friendship survived on iMessage (daily, relentless), long weekends home, summers (partial), and occasional visits to NYC during Juilliard. They watched his world get bigger from the outside. The bond didn't weaken — it just became something they maintained rather than something proximity gave them for free. Now he's home and they're together again, making up for lost time.

See TOMMY.md for full profile. Voice summary:

**Blunt. Curses freely. Communicates in jokes and insults. Understated, not sitcom.**

**Texting:** Short. Blunt. Profane. No capitals, minimal punctuation.

| Sample | Context |
|--------|---------|
| *"you still owe me twenty bucks"* | Running joke |
| *"dude come over im bored as hell"* | Wanting company |

**In SB Chat:** Roasting energy. Fast. Gets the last word or tries to. Usually funnier than Cody. *"No."* as complete response to a Cody plan. *"Sure"* meaning it's happening.

**The anchor.** Known Alex since kindergarten. Been to the compound hundreds of times. Seen The Circle. Never made a thing of it. Tommy predates everything else. Tommy doesn't move.

## JAKE CAHILL — The Quiet One

**Age:** 21  
**Height:** 5'8"  
**Appearance:** Sandy brown hair, green eyes, lean. Quietest of the group.

**Family:** Cahills breed thoroughbreds. Cahill Farm — ~800 acres outside Versailles, racing stock, some have run at Keeneland and Churchill Downs. Dad: **Brent Cahill** — third-generation horseman, Keeneland board member, quiet authority in the county. Mom: **Lydia Cahill** — horse family herself (née Ashby), manages the breeding operation. Family lake house on Herrington Lake — used for group weekends.

**Personality:** Reliable to the point of invisibility. Shows up, does the thing, doesn't need credit. Reads situations before reacting. Can ride anything — grew up on horseback before he could read. Knows bloodlines the way Alex knows chord progressions. Would rather be around horses than people, but would never say that.

**Speaking:** Short answers. Not shy — economical.

| Sample | Context |
|--------|---------|
| *"Yeah."* | Agreement |
| *"Nah, that one pulls left."* | On a horse |
| *"I'll drive."* | Offering to take charge |

Longer when talking about horses or land — that's where he opens up.

**Texting:** Minimal, factual, no emoji. Texts like he talks. Single responses where most would text paragraphs.

**In SB Chat:** Barely posts. When he does it's about horses, land, or logistics. *"I'll drive."* No emoji. Nobody doubts him. His input on Keeneland is authoritative.

**With Alex:** The one who doesn't need to fill silence. Entire afternoons on horseback without talking. Comfortable. Taught Alex most of what he knows about riding. Unimpressed by fame because horses don't care who your grandmother is.

**Current:** Working on the family farm. Might do equine science at UK (University of Kentucky). Not rushing it. Same pace as everything else he does.

**What he is:** Solid. Quiet depth. The one you want with you when something needs doing.

## CODY ASHFORD — The Loud One

**Age:** 21  
**Height:** 5'10"  
**Build:** Broad-shouldered, dark hair, ruddy complexion. Built like a rugby prop.  
**Personality:** Loudest person in any room, always has been.

**Family:** Dad: **Warren Ashford** — Senior VP at Woodford Reserve (Brown-Forman), one of the more prominent bourbon executives in Kentucky. Not Hatfield money but solidly upper-class bourbon industry. Mom: **Trish Ashford** — real estate, sells the expensive properties in Woodford and Fayette County, knows everyone. Big house on acreage between Versailles and Midway.

**Personality:** Opinions about everything. Hunting, bourbon, trucks, sports, politics (badly). Generous, loyal, would fight anyone for his friends. Not stupid — just loud. Heart in the right place, mouth gets there first. The one most likely to say the exact wrong thing at the exact wrong time, then somehow charm his way out of it.

**Speaking:** Strong opinions. Enthusiastic. High volume. Declarations.

| Sample | Context |
|--------|---------|
| *"I'm telling you right now—"* | Starting many sentences |
| *"Wait, you're telling me Beyoncé was just IN YOUR KITCHEN?"* | Processing Alex's world |
| *"That's not right, man."* | Disagreement |

**Texting:** Loud. Declarative. Texts in bursts — five messages where one would do. All caps when excited.

| Sample | Context |
|--------|---------|
| *"I'M TELLING YOU RIGHT NOW"* | Strong emotion |
| *"NOT happening"* | Disagreement |
| Multiple rapid messages | Excitement or stress |

**In SB Chat:** Highest volume by a factor of three. Reports on Woodford County events nobody asked about. First to post, last to drop a thread. "NOT Cody" driving allocation is the group's longest-running joke. Cody doesn't find it funny.

**With Alex:** Treats Alex's world as content. Not starstruck — entertained. *"Wait, you're telling me Beyoncé was just IN YOUR KITCHEN?"* Difference from Tommy: Tommy is unbothered because he genuinely doesn't care. Cody is unbothered because he processes everything as comedy.

**Current:** Working for his dad at Brown-Forman. Sales side, entry level. Talks about it like he's running the company.

**What he is:** Loyal, loud, funny in bursts, sometimes tactless, genuinely generous. The one who commits fully to plans and then improvises when they fall apart. The entertainment in the group.

## DANNY HARLAN — The Observer

**Age:** 21  
**Height:** 5'9"  
**Appearance:** Lean, dark features, wire-framed glasses he hates but needs. Looks ten years older — something about the stillness.

**Family:** Mom: **Dr. Anne Harlan** — orthopedic surgeon at Baptist Health Lexington, one of the best in the region. Dad: **Roy Harlan** — manages a cattle operation (~600 acres, Jessamine County), practical, old-school. Money from both sides but they don't show it. Danny's the only child. House is nice but not showy.

**Personality:** Driest humour in the group after Tommy, but different — Tommy is blunt-dry, Danny is observational-dry. Sees the absurd in everything and points it out once, quietly. Pre-med at Centre College (Danville, ~30 min south). Smart in the way that doesn't perform. The one who'd notice something's off with Alex before Alex admitted it.

**Speaking:** Dry observations delivered as though they aren't jokes until you've processed them. Single sentences. Thoughtful pauses.

| Sample | Context |
|--------|---------|
| *"Fascinating."* | Means the opposite |
| *"That seems like a good decision."* | Means it absolutely isn't |
| *"I see."* | He sees everything |

**Texting:** Proper punctuation, lowercase, dry. One message where Cody sends five. The message is funnier.

**In SB Chat:** One message per thread. Complete sentence. Arrives late, reads back, finds the exact right observation. *"That seems like it'll go well"* about a Cody plan (means it absolutely won't). The chat waits for Danny's take without realising it.

**With Alex:** The one who asks the real question eventually. Not a pusher — waits three hours, then over a beer: *"So are you actually okay, or are you doing that thing where you say you're fine."* Known Alex since kindergarten. Not intimidated by any of it. Just... paying attention.

**Current:** Centre College, pre-med. Home most weekends. Studies hard, doesn't talk about it.

**What he is:** The observer. The one who notices. The one who'll ask the hard question when it matters. Loyal in the way that means he pays actual attention.

## VOICE CONSTRUCTION FOR SAYRE SCENES

### Group Chat (Sayre Boys iMessage ~7 people)

**What happens:** Spontaneous. Fast. Roasting energy. High bar for what counts as funny. Inside jokes from 20 years of friendship.

**Cast rotation:** Not just Tommy, Cody, Jake, Danny. Include whoever else is in the chat. Nobody should dominate.

**Example thread pace:**
1. One person suggests a plan
2. Cody immediately responds with too much enthusiasm
3. Danny's one-line observation about why the plan is doomed
4. Tommy: *"No."*
5. Jake: *"I'll drive."*
6. Plan either solidifies or collapses

### In-Person (Compound, Lake Weekends, County Fair)

**Register shifts:** More verbal. Teasing is physical too — shoulder shoves, mock-fighting. The intimacy of 21 years.

**Conversational pattern:** A lot of back-and-forth. Sitting around. Not intense. Horsing around (literally, sometimes).

**When Alex is present:** No change. They treat him like they always have. The only difference is five years of catching up layered on top of 16 years of baseline.

### Outside Woodford County (When They Travel)

The operation hits at scale. Paps, tabloid interest, the companion identification machine. They are not prepared. Alex is protective of them in this context in a way he isn't about himself.

## CRITICAL NOTES

**Do not write Woodford County as rural/country/Appalachian.** Woodford County is the Surrey of Kentucky. Horse country, polo, Keeneland, thoroughbred farms, old money. One of the wealthiest agricultural regions in America.

**Do not write these as unsophisticated.** Sayre School K-12. University of Kentucky. Centre College. Educated. Traveled (though some choose not to). Competent in their world.

**Do not write them as in awe of Alex's world.** They grew up inside it. Stevie Nicks having coffee is Tuesday. The compound is just where their friend lives.

**Do not write Tommy as a sidekick.** He is an equal. Full stop. The anchor. The one constant.

**Do not write Jake as sheltered.** He chooses Woodford County. That's different from being confined to it.

**Do not write Cody as stupid.** He's loud and his mouth gets there first, but he's not dumb. He works in bourbon industry sales. He's competent.

**Do not write Danny as just observational.** He's pre-med. He pays attention to people because he's genuinely interested in understanding them. That's not a character quirk — that's who he is.
